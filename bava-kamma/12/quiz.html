QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, when is one liable for putting a jug in רשות הרבים?
A. Always — it is never normal to leave things there
B. Only when it fills the entire public domain (בִּמְמַלָּא אֶת כָּל רְשׁוּת הָרַבִּים)
C. Only at a corner (קֶרֶן זָוִית)
D. Only if someone trips at night
Correct: B — Rav: liable only if the jug fills the entire road. Shmuel adds the corner case; R' Lazar broadens further.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Shmuel add to Rav's position?
A. He requires nightfall as well
B. He extends liability to the case where the jug is at a corner — נְתוּנָה עַל קֶרֶן זָוִית
C. He limits liability to weekdays
D. He exempts the owner if it was a clay jug
Correct: B — Shmuel: אוֹ בִּמְמַלָּא אֶת כָּל רְשׁוּת הָרַבִּים אוֹ עַד שֶׁתְּהֵא נְתוּנָה עַל קֶרֶן זָוִית.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why doesn't the daf simply say: pick up the jug and put it down somewhere else?
A. Because moving it counts as transferring on Shabbat
B. Because אִם יִטְלֶינָּהּ מִיכָּן וְיִתְנֶינָּהּ כָּאן נַעֲשֶׂה בוֹר — relocating just creates a new pit
C. Because clay vessels cannot be moved once placed
D. Because only the rabbis are permitted to move public hazards
Correct: B — Picking it up and replacing it elsewhere just creates a fresh בור. The daf suggests breaking it (with a stick or by stepping) instead.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is R' Yose's principle drawn from the irrigation, chrysargyros, and Roman-billet cases?
A. One may always pay a bribe to avoid Roman taxes
B. Once damage has reached you, you may not divert it onto someone else; before it reaches you, you may
C. Water damage and tax damage are halakhically identical
D. Roman soldiers are exempt from civil liability
Correct: B — Before the flood/collector/soldiers arrive, you may redirect them. Once they have landed on you, you may not push the harm to a neighbor.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Natan in the case of an ox pushing another ox into a pit (when the attacker is מועד), how is liability split?
A. The ox owner pays everything
B. The pit owner pays everything
C. Each pays half (זה נותן מחצה וזה נותן מחצה)
D. The ox owner pays a quarter and the pit owner three quarters
Correct: C — R' Natan: בְּמוּעָד זֶה נוֹתֵן מֶחֱצָה וְזֶה נוֹתֵן מֶחֱצָה — half and half. The fractions shift in the תם case.

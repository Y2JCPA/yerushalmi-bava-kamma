📖 Overview — Where 'Ox' Stops and Animal Begins

אִם כֵן לָמָּה נֶאֱמַר שׁוֹר אוֹ חֲמוֹר. אֶלָּא שֶׁדִּיבֵּר הַכָּתוּב בַּהוֹוֶה
The sugya: The end of perek 5 turns the spotlight on a single Torah word: 'shor.' Why does the verse say 'ox or donkey' if the rules apply to all animals? The Yerushalmi answers with a brilliant pivot: שֶׁדִּיבֵּר הַכָּתוּב בַּהוֹוֶה — Scripture spoke in the typical case. Then the daf surveys eight halakhot where 'shor' really means 'any beheima,' weighing in on kilayim of similar birds. Perek 6 then opens with the corral — when does proper locking exempt the owner from his flock's damage?

💬 Vessels in the Pit — Three Voices

Why are vessels exempt even when the animal is liable?
Logic
If a damage-pit (less than 10 tefachim) is liable for vessels, surely a death-pit (10+) should be — kal va-chomer!
Pasuk
וְנָפַל שָׁמָּה שׁוֹר אוֹ חֲמוֹר — only ox or donkey, not 'an ox with its equipment.' The Torah blocks the kal va-chomer.
Shmuel
Vessels are only exempt when the animal got disoriented mid-air. If they hit the floor — חַיָיב.
R' Y / RL
Even if they hit the floor, פָּטוּר. דֶּרֶךְ נְפִילָה פָטְרָה תוֹרָה — the Torah exempted the entire act of falling, regardless of where the damage happens.
Yerushalmi style: A clean halakhic principle (vessels are exempt) is shown to rest on two layers — the pasuk's wording and the conceptual frame of דֶּרֶךְ נְפִילָה.

🐂 Why Specifically 'Shor o Chamor'?

The Mishnah lists eight halakhot where 'shor' covers every animal.

📜 Pit / כפל / השב אבדה

  • שור או חמור — broad coverage
  • מִשּׁוֹר עַד חֲמוֹר — keifel applies
  • הָשֵׁב תְּשִׁיבֵם — return any lost animal

💪 פריקה / חסימה

  • עֲזוֹב תַּעֲזוֹב — unload any beheima
  • לֹא תַחְסוֹם שׁוֹר בְּדִישׁוֹ — applies to all

🚫 כלאיים / שבת / סיני

  • בְּהֶמְתְּךָ לֹא תַרְבִּיעַ
  • לְמַעַן יָנוּחַ שׁוֹרְךָ וַחֲמוֹרֶךָ
  • Animals separated for Mattan Torah
The hermeneutic move: שֶׁדִּיבֵּר הַכָּתוּב בַּהוֹוֶה — when Torah names the most common case, the rule extends to all parallel members of the category. 'Ox' is shorthand, not exclusion.

📖 The Mishnah 6:1 — Locked Corrals

When does shemira shift the loss?
הַכּוֹנֵס צֹאן לַדִּיר וְנָעַל בְּפָנֶיהָ כָּרָאוּי וְיָצְתָה וְהִזִּיקָה חַיָיב
Three cases: 1. Locked properly, animal escaped on its own — חַיָיב. 2. Wall broken at night, or robbers broke in and animal escaped — פָּטוּר. 3. Robbers actively let them out — the robbers bear liability.
The threshold: Even 'as he should' locking isn't enough if the animal still gets out and damages — because the Torah's standard of shemira for keren is higher. Each אב has its own bar of shmira.

🔄 The Three-Way Argument on the Corral

Is the Mishnah following one Tanna or all?
1️⃣ R' La in the name of R' Yannai: this Mishnah reflects a מַחֲלוֹקֶת — only one Tanna's view.
↓
2️⃣ R' Eleazar: דִּבְרֵי הַכֹּל — everyone holds this.
↓
3️⃣ R' La's reasoning: shemira for the body is only written for keren; for foot/tooth, the standard differs.
↓
4️⃣ Reish Lakish: בְּמַחֲלוֹקֶת — agreed it's contested.
↓
5️⃣ Some say: it follows R' Eliezer ben Yaakov, who holds בֵּין כָּךְ וּבֵין כָּךְ פָּטוּר — exempt regardless.
What's at stake: Whether normal locking is enough — or whether keren's higher standard requires more — depends on whose Mishnah you're reading. The Yerushalmi keeps the question alive rather than collapse it.

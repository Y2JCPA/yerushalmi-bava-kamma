Yerushalmi Bava Kamma 18 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What two-sided argument does R' Yannai attribute to R' Meir?
A. Either the ox is yours and you give it as payment, or it isn't and you let me collect from it
B. Either you pay full damage or no damage
C. Either you accept the kal va-chomer or reject the Mishnah
D. Either the ox is mu'ad or it is tam
Correct: A — שֶׁלָּךְ הוּא תְּנֵיהוּ לוֹ. אֵינוֹ שֶׁלָּךְ תְּנֵיהוּ לִי שֶׁאַגְבֶּנּוּ — R' Meir's מה נפשך leaves the owner no way out.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does R' Yudan answer in defense of R' Yehudah's view?
A. The ox truly belongs to the נזק
B. The ox was given over for damages but remains כְּמַשְׁכּוֹן בְּיָדוֹ — only a pledge
C. There is no liability at all
D. The ox is hekdesh
Correct: B — R' Yudan re-reads R' Eleazar: שמירת נזיקין כשמירת קניין means a pledge-like custody, not full ownership.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Meir, who keeps the increase if the goring ox rises in value (הוקיר)?
A. The first owner alone
B. The נזק (second owner) alone
C. Both, since they are partners
D. Hekdesh
Correct: C — R' Yochanan lists this as one of four test cases. R' Meir treats them as joint owners, so הוקרה לשניהם.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Yochanan describe R' Yehudah and R' Shimon on 'קנה'?
A. They flatly disagree
B. Both say קנה, but R' Yehudah means living and dead, R' Shimon means only the dead
C. Both say he did not acquire
D. Only R' Shimon says קנה
Correct: B — שְׁנֵיהֶן אָמְרוּ דָבָר אֶחָד — same word, different scope. R' Yehudah: לחי וגם למת; R' Shimon: למת בלבד.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In R' Eleazar's analysis of שלשה שהטילו לכיס, why might the partner with less capital still get an equal share?
A. Because the small investor turns inventory over quickly while the big investor is slow to sell
B. Because R' Bun says so explicitly
C. Because precious stones cannot be divided
D. Because of גזירה שווה
Correct: A — אֲנָא פְּרַגְמָטַּיָּא דִידִי קָלִיל וַאֲנַא הֲפַךְ וּמִתְהַפֵּךְ בְּדִידִי וּמַטִּי בָךְ — small inventory means fast turnover, which earns an equal share.

📖 Overview — The Opening Framework of Nezikin

אַרְבָּעָה אָבוֹת נְזִיקִין: הַשּׁוֹר וְהַבּוֹר וְהַמַּבְעֶה וְהַהֶבְעֵר
The sugya: The Mishnah opens with four primary categories of damage. The Yerushalmi immediately asks what each av means in the pesukim, then expands the conversation: if there are more liability categories in other beraitot, why does our Mishnah choose exactly four?

🧱 The Four Avot

The Mishnah gives the master list before the Yerushalmi defines the details.

🐂 השור

  • Yerushalmi: זה הקרן
  • Source: כי יגוף שור איש את שור רעהו
  • Starts with tam, then asks for mu'ad

🕳️ הבור

  • A hazard created in public space
  • Source: כי יפתח איש בור
  • Owner of the pit pays

🌾 המבעה

  • Yerushalmi connects it to רגל and שן
  • רגל: ושלח את בעירה
  • שן: והיה לבער

🔥 ההבער

  • Fire damage
  • Contrasted with moving/living categories
  • Still included because דרכו להזיק
Key move: The Mishnah is not just listing cases. It is building a liability taxonomy.

💬 Four or Thirteen?

The Yerushalmi compares our Mishnah with R' Chiyya's larger list.
M
Our Mishnah taught: אַרְבָּעָה אָבוֹת נְזִיקִין — four primary categories.
RḤ
But R' Chiyya taught thirteen: damages, pain, medical costs, loss of work, shame, and the watchmen categories.
Y
Answer: our Mishnah is about property-damage frameworks, while R' Chiyya includes bodily injury and custodial liabilities too.
Practical reading: The number depends on what universe you are counting. The Mishnah narrows to core damage categories. R' Chiyya broadens to liability categories.

🧠 The Mishnah's Logic

Why not learn all damage categories from one example?
1️⃣ Each av has a feature the others do not fully share.
↓
2️⃣ Living/moving damage is unlike a stationary pit.
↓
3️⃣ A pit is unlike fire, which spreads differently.
↓
4️⃣ The shared denominator is: דרכן להזיק ושמירתן עליך.
↓
5️⃣ Therefore: when they damage, the mazikin pays במיטב הארץ.
Bottom line: The daf teaches how Nezikin starts: define the category, find its Torah source, then test what it can and cannot teach.

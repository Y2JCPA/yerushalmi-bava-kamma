📖 Overview — Watchmen, Levels of Guarding, and the Cow's Calf

מְסָרוֹ לְשׁוֹמֵר חִנָּם וְלַשּׁוֹאֵל וְלַנּוֹשֵׂא שָׂכָר וְהַשּׂוֹכֵר נִכְנְסוּ תַחַת הַבְּעָלִים.
The sugya: Daf 23 closes Chapter 4 with the topic of handing the bull to a guardian and the level of שמירה required, then opens Chapter 5 with the famous mystery of the gored cow and her calf — a sugya that probes whether monetary law follows majority probability.

🛡️ Levels of Guarding

R' Eleazar maps the dispute between R' Meir and R' Yehudah onto the שומרים grid.

🆓 R' Meir

  • שמירת נזקין כשמירת שומר חינם
  • Unpaid trustee level guarding suffices
  • Looser standard — easier to be patur

💰 R' Yehudah

  • שמירת נזקין כשומר שכר
  • Paid trustee level guarding required
  • Stricter — harder to escape liability

🔪 R' Eliezer

  • A muad has only one שמירה
  • אין לו שמירה אלא סכין
  • Slaughter is the only adequate guard

⚖️ Practical test

  • Even iron walls aren't enough alone
  • אין משערין אלא בגופו
  • Personal capacity to watch is decisive
Key move: The Yerushalmi reads the Mishnah's dispute about a tied-down ox as a fight over which שומר category sets the bar for damage-prevention.

🐄 The Cow With Her Calf

A bull gored a cow; her fetus is found beside her.
משנה: שור שנגח את הפרה ונמצא עוברה בצידה
↓
Was the calf already born — or did the goring cause the miscarriage?
↓
If known
Pay full half-damage
If doubtful
Half נזק for cow + רביע for calf
Yerushalmi probe
Why not follow רוב — most cows don't miscarry?
↓
Conclusion: monetary law generally doesn't follow majority — but R' Yose cites R' Acha's camel-in-heat case as a counter-example.
Yerushalmi tension: זאת אומרת שלא הילכו במידת הדין בממון אחר הרוב — but R' Acha's camel baraita shows they sometimes did.

💬 R' Yannai vs R' Yose — How Does the Half + Quarter Work?

Two structural readings of the Mishnah's split payment.
RY
R' Yannai: משלם חצי נזק מן הפרה ובעל הוולדות עולה לבעל הפרה רביע — owner pays half from the cow value, and the calf-owner kicks back a quarter to the cow-owner.
RY2
R' Yose: each pays half — half for the cow, half for the calf, treated as separate damage events.
Q
What's the practical difference? מתו הוולדות — if the calves died after the goring.
A
On R' Yannai's reading the cow-owner can say 'I didn't want them — take them as they are.' On the other reading, each pays his own share regardless.
Levi's traditions: Reish Lakish concludes: כל אילין שמועתא דלוי אינון — all these readings come from Levi's school.

🐪 The Camel-in-Heat — Following the Rov

R' Yose's counter-example to the cow-and-calf principle.
1️⃣ A camel in heat (גמל האוחר) wandered among other camels.
↓
2️⃣ One of them is found dead.
↓
3️⃣ Ruling: the camel-in-heat is liable — בידוע שזה הרגו.
↓
4️⃣ Reasoning: the overwhelming probability is that the agitated camel did it.
↓
5️⃣ Therefore: monetary law sometimes does follow רוב — the cow case is the exception, not the rule.
Bottom line: R' Avun bar Cahana asks: if it were certain, would each pay half? R' Immi answers: yes — the Mishnah deals with a muad, and the half is a sale-of-foot logic.

Yerushalmi Bava Kamma 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Eleazar, what guarding level does R' Meir require for damages?
A. שומר שכר
B. שומר חינם
C. שואל
D. שוכר
Correct: B — R' Eleazar: R' Meir's standard is the שומר חינם level; R' Yehudah requires the שומר שכר level.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does R' Eliezer say one guards a notorious bull?
A. With an iron wall
B. Only with the knife — slaughter
C. By chaining it to the ground
D. By selling it
Correct: B — אין לו שמירה אלא סכין — the only adequate guarding for a muad is to slaughter it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the cow-and-calf case, why doesn't the Mishnah pay full damages for the calf?
A. The calf is exempt
B. It's uncertain whether the goring caused the miscarriage
C. Calves aren't real damages
D. The cow died first
Correct: B — Because of the doubt about timing, the Mishnah pays only a רביע for the calf — half the half-damage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does R' Acha's camel-in-heat baraita prove?
A. Camels are not liable
B. Monetary law sometimes does follow רוב
C. Heat is an exempt force majeure
D. Witnesses are required
Correct: B — R' Yose cites it to show that in some monetary cases the law does apply majority probability.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Yannai and R' Yose's readings of the Mishnah?
A. The amount of damages
B. Whether witnesses are needed
C. What happens if the calves die after the goring
D. Whether the cow is muad or tam
Correct: C — On R' Yannai's reading the cow-owner can refuse the dead calves; on R' Yose's, each pays his share regardless.

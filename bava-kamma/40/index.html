📖 Overview — Reaching the Victim and Returning the Object

הַגּוֹזֵל אֶת חֲבֵירוֹ שָׁוֶה פְרוּטָה וְנִשְׁבַּע לוֹ יוֹלִיכֶנּוּ אַחֲרָיו אֲפִילוּ לְמָדַי
The sugya: Daf 40 closes the ערכין discussion (R' Hoshaya teaches that foreclosure on movables for valuations is rabbinic, not biblical) and then opens Mishnah 9:6: the robber who took something worth a peruṭa and swore falsely must pursue the victim even to Media. The Yerushalmi works out the chain of payments (קרן, חומש, אשם), the rules for who counts as a valid pickup-agent, and the case of a deposit confessed at the moment of death — culminating in Rav's ruling on R' Abba bar Chana's wife: אֵין אָדָם מָצוּי לְשַׁקֵּר בִּשְׁעַת מִיתָה.

🏷️ Closing the ערכין Sugya — Movables Are Only Rabbinic

זֹאת אוֹמֶרֶת שֶׁאֵין שָׁמִין עֲרָכִין מִן הַמִּטַּלְטְלַין דְּבַר תּוֹרָה אֶלָּא מִדִּבְרֵיהֶן
R' Hoshaya in the name of Shmuel bar Abba: The Mishnah excludes the wife and children's clothing from valuation foreclosure. The Yerushalmi probes: what if someone said 'my valuation on me on condition that you not foreclose on my wife's and children's clothing'? R' Nasa: he can stipulate against any specific item. R' Hoshaya draws the inference: זֹאת אוֹמֶרֶת שֶׁאֵין שָׁמִין עֲרָכִין מִן הַמִּטַּלְטְלַין דְּבַר תּוֹרָה אֶלָּא מִדִּבְרֵיהֶן — biblically, valuations are foreclosed only from real estate; movables are rabbinic.

🚶 The Robber Who Swore Falsely — Pursuit to Media

Mishnah 9:6 and the chain of consequences.
1️⃣ Robber took something worth at least a פְּרוּטָה and swore falsely denying it.
↓
2️⃣ He must personally deliver the object to the victim — אֲפִילוּ לְמָדַי (even to Media).
↓
3️⃣ He may not hand it to the victim's son or agent — only to שְׁלִיחַ בֵּית דִּין.
↓
4️⃣ If the victim has died, the robber repays the heirs.
↓
5️⃣ He pays קרן + חומש + אשם; if the principal drops below a peruṭa via partial payment, the chase ends.
Why so strict: The false oath converted a civil debt into a personal religious obligation. The robber must literally place the object back in the victim's hand.

💸 The חומש-on-חומש Cascade

What happens when the robber swears again on the fifth.
StageWhat happensConsequence
1. Initial robbery + false oathPays קֶרֶן + חוֹמֶשׁ + אָשָׁםStandard 9:6 setup
2. Pays principal but swears falsely on the fifthהֲרֵי זֶה מְשַׁלֵּם חוֹמֶשׁ עַל חוֹמֶשׁNew chumash on the unpaid chumash
3. R' Yonatan's readingבִּנְתִינַת הַחוֹמֶשׁ נַעֲשֶׂה חוֹמֶשׁ קֶרֶןEach unpaid fifth becomes a fresh principal
4. Cascade ends when…עַד שֶׁיִּתְמָעֵט חוֹמֶשׁ הָאַחֲרוֹן פָּחוֹת מִשָּׁוֶה פְרוּטָהBelow peruṭa = no obligation
R' Yonatan's principle: Each unpaid חומש legally morphs into a new קרן the moment the robber swears falsely on it. The fifth piggybacks on its own fifth, recursively, until the residue dips below a peruṭa.

🤝 Deposits From Women, Slaves, and Minors

The Tannaitic rule on accepting deposits from those without independent legal standing.

👩 From a woman

  • Should not accept
  • If accepted: return to her
  • If she died: return to her husband

🧑 From a slave

  • Should not accept
  • If accepted: return to him
  • If he died: return to his master

👶 From a minor

  • Should not accept
  • If accepted: return to him
  • If he died: return to his father as סְגוּלָּה (safe investment)

💬 Deathbed declaration

  • If the depositor named someone at death: יַעֲשֶׂה פֵירוּשׁ לְפֵירוּשׁוֹ
  • R' Zebida: only with witnesses
  • Living-out their stated intent
Logic of the defaults: The presumption is that whatever each dependent class held actually belongs to the responsible party (husband, master, father). A deathbed naming overrides the default — but only if witnessed.

💍 The Story: R' Abba bar Chana's Wife

Rav's ruling on dying-words testimony.
R' Abba bar Chana's wife is dying
↓
She declares: אָהֵן קִידּוּשָׁא דִבְרַתִּי
(these rings belong to my daughter)
↓
Husband's claim
לֵית הוּא אֶלָּא דִידִי
they're mine
Wife's claim
they belong to the daughter
Case before Rav
Rav rules for the wife's words
↓
🎓 אֵין אָדָם מָצוּי לְשַׁקֵּר בִּשְׁעַת מִיתָה — people do not lie at death.
Why this matters: Rav's principle becomes a foundational rule for halakhic deathbed testimony: a person at the threshold of death is presumed credible. The case-law is built right into the daf.

📖 Overview — Consent, Coercion, and the Robbed Object

הָאוֹמֵר סַמֵּא אֵת עֵינִי קְטַע אֶת יָדִי שַׁבֵּר אֶת רַגְלִי חַיָיב עַל מְנָת לִפְטוֹר חַיָיב
The sugya: The daf closes Chapter 8 with the laws of consenting to one's own injury (סמא את עיני) and being coerced by Gentiles to hand over a fellow's money. It then opens Chapter 9: the foundational principle that a robber pays the value of the object at the time of the gezeilah, even if it has since been transformed into utensils or garments. The amoraim debate whether bleaching wool counts as a שינוי that locks in the gazlan's payment.

👁️ Body vs. Property — Consent Cuts Differently

The Mishnah distinguishes between damage to the body and damage to property.

🩸 'Blind my eye' (גופו)

  • סַמֵּא אֵת עֵינִי → liable
  • Even עַל מְנָת לִפְטוֹר → still liable
  • A person cannot waive bodily damage

👕 'Tear my garment' (ממונו)

  • קְרַע אֶת כְּסוּתִי → liable
  • But עַל מְנָת לִפְטוֹר → exempt
  • Property rights can be waived

👥 To a third party

  • עֲשֵׂה כֵן לְאִישׁ פְּלוֹנִי
  • Even on condition to be exempt → liable
  • Body and money — no waiver works
Key move: Consent shifts liability for property but not for body. And no one can grant exemption on someone else's behalf.

💬 Does 'Yes' Mean 'Yes' or 'No'?

R' Lazar and Reish Laqish split on how to read the Mishnah's 'on condition'.
RL
R' Lazar: The Mishnah is talking about a 'yes' that really means 'no' (בָּהֵן שֶׁהוּא כְלָאו). But a sincere 'yes' that means 'yes' — exempt.
RŠ
Reish Laqish: The Mishnah is the genuine 'yes' (בָּהֵן שֶׁהוּא כְהֵין). But a 'yes' that means 'no' — even more so, liable.
Y
Implication: R' Lazar is concerned with פְּגַם מִשְׁפָּחָה — the family's loss of standing. Reish Laqish implies one assesses bushet for relatives.
Why it matters: The dispute decides whether a victim's permission can ever truly exempt the מזיק. R' Lazar reads consent narrowly; Reish Laqish reads it more literally.

🛡️ Coerced by Gentiles — When Are You Liable?

A baraita on a Jew forced to give over another's money.
1️⃣ Gentiles forcibly take a fellow's money in his presence → פָּטוּר.
↓
2️⃣ He actively handed it over (נָטַל וְנָתַן לָהֶם) → חַיָיב.
↓
3️⃣ R' Yose qualifies: this is when they demanded 'money' generically (מָמוֹן סְתָם).
↓
4️⃣ But if they specified a particular person's money (מָמוֹן פְּלוֹנִי) — even if he handed it over, exempt.
↓
5️⃣ Logic: when they targeted that specific money, his hand-over did not change the outcome.
Practical reading: Liability tracks how much added harm the actor caused. Specificity by the coercer breaks the chain of responsibility.

🪵 Mishnah 9:1 — The Robber Pays at Time of Theft

When the gazlan transforms the object, what does he pay?
גָּזַל עֵצִים וַעֲשָׂאָן כֵּלִים
(robbed wood, made utensils)
↓
מְשַׁלֵּם כְּשָׁעַת הַגְּזֵילָה
Pays the value at the time of the robbery
↓
Pregnant cow → calved
Pays cow ready to calve
Sheep with wool → shorn
Pays sheep ready to shear
Became pregnant with him → calved
Pays value at time of gezeilah
↓
זֶה הַכְּלָל כָּל הַגַּזְלָנִין מְשַׁלְּמִין כְּשָׁעַת הַגְּזֵילָה
The principle: Once שינוי kicks in, the original owner can no longer demand the transformed object. The gazlan pays the snapshot price from the moment of theft.

🧼 Is Bleaching a שינוי?

The Yerushalmi reconciles two contradictory rulings.
PositionCaseResult
לִיבּוּן כְּשִׁינּוּיHe turned it into צוּפִים (wool flakes)Bleaching counts as change
אֵין לִיבּוּן כְּשִׁינּוּיHe left it as-is (just bleached)Bleaching alone does not change ownership
R' YudanEither way: קַל הוּא שֶׁהֵיקֵלוּ בְגַזְלָןIt is a leniency for the robber to pay כשעת הגזילה
שֶׁלַח (raw hide) cleanedCan you say it became 'flakes'?Yet again — leniency for the gazlan
Bottom line: The amoraim navigate between פְּגָם and שִׁינּוּי. R' Yudan steps back and says the entire framework is a leniency — protect the gazlan from ever-changing valuations by freezing the price at the gezeilah moment.

📖 Overview — The Final Daf: Returns, Trust, and Tradesmen

הָאוֹמֵר לַחֲבֵירוֹ גְּזַלְתִּיךָ וְהִלְוִיתַנִי הִפְקַדְתָּ אֶצְלִי וְאֵינוֹ יוֹדֵעַ אִם הֶחֱזַרְתִּי לָךְ וְאִם לֹא הֶחֱזַרְתִּי לָךְ חַיָיב לְשַׁלֵּם
The sugya: The closing daf of the masechet ties together how a robber returns property, how courts adjudicate uncertain admissions, and how craftsmen handle the leftover materials of clients. The sugya moves from a robbed field seized by veterans, through the famous distinction between 'I don't know if I returned' (liable) and 'I don't know if I robbed' (exempt), through a stolen lamb that must be re-counted, to the small offcuts of tailors, washers, and woodworkers. The masechet ends with the precise measurements of a tailor's needle.

🌾 Robbed Field Seized by Veterans

Three scenarios, three rulings.

🪖 Provincial Plague (מַכַּת מְדִינָה)

  • הֲרֵי שֶׁלָּךְ לְפָנֶיךָ
  • Robber says: 'Here is your field'
  • Robber not liable for replacement

👤 Because of the Robber

  • חַיָיב לְהַעֲמִיד לוֹ שָׂדֶה
  • R' Yochanan: real estate cannot be 'robbed' — so why liable?
  • קְנָס קְנָסוּהוּ — it is a fine

🌊 River Washed It Away

  • הֲרֵי שֶׁלָּךְ לְפָנֶיךָ
  • Treated as natural disaster
  • No liability for replacement
R' Yehoshua b. Levi: אֵין אָדָם נִתְפָּשׂ עַל חֲבֵירוֹ — one isn't held for another's debt, except annona and head-tax.

⚖️ Two Kinds of 'I Don't Know'

The Mishnah distinguishes uncertainty about the return from uncertainty about the act.
M
If he says גְּזַלְתִּיךָ ... וְאֵינוֹ יוֹדֵעַ אִם הֶחֱזַרְתִּי לָךְ — he is liable to pay.
M
But if he says אֵינִי יוֹדֵעַ אִם גְּזַלְתִּיךָ — he is exempt.
RY
R' Yochanan: this is for one who wants to clear himself לָצֵאת יְדֵי שָׁמַיִם — before Heaven.
RJ
R' Jeremiah: he must pay as a matter of strict law.
RH
Rav Huna: tell him — אַתְּ לֵית יְדַע אָהֵן יְדַע — you don't know, but the other party does.
Rule of thumb: Certainty about the wrong + uncertainty about repair = liable. Uncertainty about the wrong itself = exempt.

🐑 Stolen Lamb Returned to the Flock

Must the owners be notified of the return?
Thief stole a lamb and put it back
↓
Did the owners know about the theft?
↓
R' Yochanan: knew of theft
Must be told of return
R' Yochanan: did NOT know
No notification needed — count suffices
Reish Lakish
Always need notification of return
R' Eleazar: like a בָּרְחָא (bell-wether/staff/pandura)
No notification needed
↓
Mishnah: if owners counted the flock and it was complete — exempt.
Curiosity: The Yerushalmi preserves three guesses for what 'בָּרְחָא' means: a shepherd's staff, a pandura (musical instrument), or the great he-goat that leads the flock.

🧵 Closing the Masechet — The Craftsmen's Offcuts

How precisely the law splits scraps between worker and client.
1️⃣ Washer: fibers he extracts are his; fibers the carder produces belong to the client. He may take three threads; black on white — he takes all.
↓
2️⃣ Abba Hoshaia of Tireh was a washer; he made his tunic of a single kind of wool דְּלָא יְהַוּוֹן בִּרְיָיתָא אָמְרִין. מִדִּידָן לְבַשׁ — so people wouldn't say 'he wears our property.'
↓
3️⃣ Tailor: if leftover thread is enough to sew with, or cloth is 3×3 fingerbreadths — return it to the client.
↓
4️⃣ Woodworker: shavings from the small plane are his; from the large plane — the client's. If working at the client's home, even chips belong to the client.
↓
5️⃣ R' Hiyya vs. Bar Qappara on the needle measure: 'a needle full' (R' Hiyya) vs. 'the stitching of a needle' (Bar Qappara) — explained as twice the basic measure. סליק מסכת בבא קמא.
Final note: The masechet ends not with grand principles but with the smallest measurements — a needle's pull, a thread's length — because justice in commerce lives in the offcuts.

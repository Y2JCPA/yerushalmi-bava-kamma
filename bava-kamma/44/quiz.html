Yerushalmi Bava Kamma 44 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is the robber who lost a robbed field 'because of the robber' liable to provide a replacement, despite אין הקרקע נגזלת?
A. The Torah explicitly says so
B. קְנָס קְנָסוּהוּ — it is a rabbinic fine
C. Land is treated as movable here
D. R' Yochanan holds land is robbable
Correct: B — R' Yochanan: although real estate cannot strictly be 'robbed,' the rabbis fined him.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the difference between the Mishnah's two confessions?
A. Both make him liable
B. Both exempt him
C. 'I don't know if I returned' = liable; 'I don't know if I robbed' = exempt
D. Only the first reaches court
Correct: C — Certainty of the wrong with uncertainty of the repayment makes him liable; uncertainty about the wrong itself does not.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, when does the lamb-returner not need the owners to know of the return?
A. Always
B. Only if the owners did not know of the theft to begin with
C. Only on Shabbat
D. Only if the lamb was famous
Correct: B — If they didn't know of the theft, counting and finding the flock complete is enough; if they knew, they must be told.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which item may be bought from anyone, anywhere, per the Mishnah?
A. Wool from shepherds
B. Wood from orchard watchmen
C. בֵּיצִים וְתַרְנוֹגַלִּין — eggs and chickens
D. Calves outside the Sharon
Correct: C — וְלוֹקְחִין בֵּיצִים וְתַרְנוֹגַלִּין מִכָּל מָקוֹם.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the disagreement between R' Hiyya and Bar Qappara about the tailor's leftover thread?
A. Whether any leftover must be returned
B. מְלֹא מַחַט (a needle full) vs. מְלֹא מְשִׁיכַת הַמַּחַט (the stitching of a needle)
C. Whether the thread belongs to the tailor at all
D. Whether the rule applies on Shabbat
Correct: B — R' Hiyya: a needle full. Bar Qappara: the stitching of a needle. The respective Houses each explained it as twice the basic measure.

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does Rav add to the Mishnah's rule of the standing beam-carrier?
A. It only applies if he is a Levi
B. It applies only when he מְמַלָּא כָּל רְשׁוּת הָרַבִּים — fills the whole road
C. It applies only at night
D. It only applies to fragile vessels
Correct: B — Rav qualifies that the rule is for someone whose beam blocks the entire public way — and once he stops, נַעֲשֶׂה כְקֶרֶן זָוִית.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is Yose HaBavli also called Yose Katontah?
A. He was physically small
B. He was the youngest of his teachers
C. שֶׁהָיָה קְטָן חֲסִידִים — he was the last of the pious
D. He learned from a small school
Correct: C — The Yerushalmi explains the nickname: he was the smallest/last of the חסידים.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When is a runner in the public domain exempt according to Yose HaBavli?
A. Always
B. When he is running on his own property
C. עֶרֶב שַׁבָּת בֵּין הַשְּׁמָשׁוֹת — Friday twilight, when running is normal
D. Only if he warned passersby
Correct: C — Running is liable because שִׁינָּה הַמִּנְהָג; but at Friday twilight, hurrying is itself the customary norm.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How do R' Yose ben Chanina and R' Chiyya reconcile on the wood-splitter case?
A. They actually disagree fundamentally
B. לֹא פְלִיגֵי — R' Yose: when he saw the worker; R' Chiyya: when he didn't see
C. R' Yose was speaking of fire damage
D. R' Chiyya speaks only about animals
Correct: B — The Yerushalmi harmonizes them by case-splitting: visibility makes the difference between liability and exemption.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is R' Yehudah's critique of R' Meir's reading of the verse?
A. R' Meir misread the Hebrew vowels
B. קִיַימְתָּה וּמָכְרוּ... וְלֹא קִיַימְתָּה וְגַם אֶת הַמֵּת יֶחֱצוּן — R' Meir upholds half the verse but ignores 'and they shall split the dead one too'
C. The verse doesn't apply to oxen
D. R' Meir's case never happens in practice
Correct: B — R' Yehudah insists every phrase of the pasuk must do halakhic work — each loses half the damage.

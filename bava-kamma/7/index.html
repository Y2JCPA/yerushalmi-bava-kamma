📖 Overview — Foot, Pebbles, and Edge Cases of הֶעָדָאָה

כָּל דָּבָר שֶׁהוּא חוּץ לְגוּפָהּ לֹא חִלְּקוּ בוֹ חֲכָמִים בֵּין בִּרְשׁוּת הַיָּחִיד בֵּין בִּרְשׁוּת הָרַבִּים לַחֲצִי כּוֹפֶר
The sugya: Chapter 2 opens by asking: how is the foot mu'ad? The Mishnah lists three sub-cases — kicking, ricocheting pebbles, and the secondary breakage when a vessel falls on another. The Yerushalmi probes each: R' Immi reframes the pebble case to the moment a person was holding the vessel, R' Abba bar Mamal introduces the two-road scenario, and R' Elazar lays down a sweeping rule — anything חוּץ לְגוּפָהּ doesn't distinguish between domains for the half-damage rule. The daf then takes up הֶעָדָאָה for things-outside-the-body, the wall-rubbing ox, and the dung case.

🦶 Mishnah 2:1 — The Foot's Three Profiles

How is רגל mu'ad?
1️⃣ Default: הַבְּהֵמָה מוּעֶדֶת לְהַלֵּךְ כְּדַרְכָּהּ וּלְשַׁבֵּר — full damage for normal walking-breakage.
↓
2️⃣ Kicking or pebbles ricocheting: half damage.
↓
3️⃣ Steps on a vessel and breaks it; pieces fall on a second vessel and break that.
↓
4️⃣ For the first vessel: full damage. For the second: half damage.
↓
5️⃣ The Mishnah is mapping different layers: direct foot-damage vs. derivative trajectories.
Key distinction: Direct contact = full. Anything launched (pebbles, fragments) = half. The animal's body is the dividing line.

💬 The Pebble Case — Where Does Liability Apply?

Three voices reframe the half-damage rule.
Q
וְאֵין דַּרְכָּהּ לְהַתִּיז צְרוֹרוֹת? — pebbles are normal walking-byproducts. Why pay anything?
RI
R' Immi: this is a case where a person was holding the vessel. If it was lying in רשות הרבים — animal walking properly, vessel sitting properly — exempt.
RB
R' Abba bar Mamal: imagine two roads — one with grasses and pebbles, one without. If the animal chose the pebble road, even in רשות הרבים, the owner is exempt.
RE
R' Elazar lays down the principle: כָּל דָּבָר שֶׁהוּא חוּץ לְגוּפָהּ לֹא חִלְּקוּ בוֹ חֲכָמִים בֵּין רשות היחיד לרשות הרבים לחצי כופר.
Yerushalmi style: Each amora reads the half-damage rule against a different fact pattern. R' Elazar then collapses the variations under one principle: outside-the-body damage doesn't distinguish domains.

🪨 Direct vs. Outside-the-Body Damage

The conceptual axis of the chapter.

🦶 Direct foot

  • הילוכה — normal walking
  • Steps on a vessel directly
  • Full damage in רשות הניזק

🪨 צרורות (pebbles)

  • Pebbles fly from under the foot
  • Half damage — חצי נזק
  • חוץ לגופה — outside the body

🍶 Falling fragments

  • Vessel A breaks → pieces hit vessel B
  • Vessel A: full damage
  • Vessel B: half damage

🛣️ Two-road case

  • Animal picks the pebble road
  • Even in רשות הרבים — exempt
  • R' Abba bar Mamal's reframe
The axis: Once the harm comes from something outside the animal's body, the half-damage rule kicks in and the domain distinction collapses.

🧱 Wall-Rubbing Ox — Can Tam Have כופר?

R' Ze'ira and R' Illa argue about הֶעָדָאָה for outside-the-body.
Q: Does הֶעָדָאָה apply to דבר שהוא חוץ לגופה?
↓
R' Ze'ira
מְקַבֶּלֶת — yes, it can be made mu'ad
R' Illa
אֵינָהּ מְקַבֶּלֶת — no
↓
Beraita: שׁוֹר שֶׁמִּתְחַכֵּךְ בַּכּוֹתֶל וְנָפַל עַל הָאָדָם וַהֲרָגוֹ — חַיָיב בַּכּוֹפֶר וּפָטוּר מִמִּיתָה
↓
Reading: there is כופר for tam? Resolution — the ox was already mu'ad to topple walls; in this case no human was at the wall when it fell.
Why this matters: The wall-rubbing case becomes the test case: if הֶעָדָאָה works on something חוץ לגופה, the beraita slots in cleanly. If not, the Yerushalmi has to read it as a standing mu'ad.

💩 R' Elazar's Dung Case

When does an animal's byproduct yield full damage?
1️⃣ הִטִּילָה גְלָלִים — the animal produced dung and damaged a vessel.
↓
2️⃣ R' Elazar: full damage.
↓
3️⃣ Counter: should we require an owner to keep a מקלוט (receptacle) handy?
↓
4️⃣ Distinction: בְּשֶׁאֵין דַּרְכָּהּ לְכֵן — when this isn't habitual, no receptacle needed.
↓
5️⃣ אֲבָל בְּשֶׁדַּרְכָּהּ לָכֵן צָרִיךְ שֶׁיְּהֵא הַמִּקְלוֹט בְּיָדוֹ — habitual behavior requires precautions.
Bottom line: Habit reshapes liability. Once the animal regularly produces dung in damaging ways, the owner's failure to bring a receptacle becomes the gateway to full damage.

📖 Overview — Pits, Layers, and Sancta

כַּמָּה שִׁיעוּרָן לְהָמִית. עֲשָׂרָה טְפָחִים. וּלְהַזִּיק. כָּל שֶׁהוּא
The sugya: The two segments of daf 5 carry an enormous amount of weight. The first explores the Mishnah's clause הִכְשַׁרְתִּי בְּמִקְצָת נִזְקוֹ: when multiple people contribute to a single pit, who pays for what kind of damage? Rebbi and R' Yitzchak split the layers of liability between death and damage, and the Yerushalmi pushes outward to ask about plastering, dune-pits, and the minimum shiur. The second segment moves to property law — which sancta fall under מעילה for the purposes of our Mishnah's reference to נכסים? — and brings the layered debate of R' Yose haGlili, Ben Azzai, and R' Shimon.

🕳️ Layered Pit — Who Pays?

First digs ten, second deepens by one tefach.
חָפַר בּוֹר עֲשָׂרָה טְפָחִים וּבָא אַחֵר וְעָמַק בּוֹ טֶפַח
↓
Beraita's plain rule: הָאַחֲרוֹן חַיָיב — the latter pays for everything
↓
Rebbi
Latter for death
Former for damage
R' Yitzchak
Latter for death
Both for damage
↓
The pit is a stack of liabilities — different layers track different harms.
Key move: הִכְשַׁרְתִּי בְּמִקְצָת נִזְקוֹ is not just a rhetorical clause. It is the legal mechanism for slicing one physical pit into multiple liability units.

💬 The Plasterer Case

Can someone become liable just by finishing another's pit?
B
חָפַר בּוֹ עֲשָׂרָה טְפָחִים וּבָא אַחֵר וְסִיָידוֹ וְכִיָירוֹ שְׁנֵיהֶן חַיָיבִין — both are liable.
Q
Just by whitewashing and lining? That doesn't deepen the pit at all!
A
When the digger told him: סוֹד אֶת הַבּוֹר הַזֶּה וּקְנֵה אוֹתוֹ — finish it and own it. The plasterer becomes the owner.
RI
R' Immi b'shem R' Elazar: שְׁמִירַת נְזָקִין כִּשְׁמִירַת קִנְיָין — the same act that triggers ownership triggers liability.
RS
R' Simon: better — read it as digging in the dunes, where any new pit is a fresh hazard, so each new digger owns his stretch.
Yerushalmi style: Two paths to make sense of the beraita: one through קנין principles, one through factual reframing. The Yerushalmi keeps both in tension.

🏛️ Sancta and מעילה — The Mishnah's Property Clause

What is included in the Mishnah's נְכָסִים שֶׁאֵין בָּהֶן מְעִילָה?
1️⃣ Beraita: הַנְּכָסִין הַלָּלוּ נִיקְנִין עִם נְכָסִין שֶׁיֵּשׁ בָּהֶן מְעִילָה.
↓
2️⃣ Rav Yehudah b'shem Shmuel: this follows R' Yose haGlili.
↓
3️⃣ R' Yose haGlili — וּמָעֲלָה מַעַל בַּיי includes קדשים קלים.
↓
4️⃣ Ben Azzai narrows: שלמים only. Abba Yose ben Dosai: only בכור.
↓
5️⃣ R' Shimon and Rav Huna split sancta by אחריות — if the owner bears risk, it's בָּעֲמִיתוֹ וְכִחֵשׁ; if not, בַּיי וְכִחֵשׁ.
The pivot: What looks like a property-law footnote is really about classifying whose property the sacred animal is. אחריות — owner's risk — becomes the line that determines which clause of the מעילה verse applies.

🐑 The Three Tannaitic Positions on Sancta

Each Tanna draws the line differently.

🌾 R' Yose haGlili

  • Drash: וּמָעֲלָה מַעַל בַּיי
  • Includes קֳדָשִׁים קַלִּין
  • Broadest scope of מעילה

🐑 Ben Azzai

  • Includes only שְׁלָמִים
  • Per Abba Yose ben Dosai: only בְּכוֹר
  • שלמים implies מעשר too; מעשר excludes בכור

⚖️ R' Shimon

  • Cuts by אחריות
  • With אחריות → בָּעֲמִיתוֹ וְכִחֵשׁ
  • Without אחריות → בַּיי וְכִחֵשׁ

🔄 Rav Huna

  • Even valid + invalid sancta
  • אחריות determines which verse applies
  • Sharpens R' Shimon's principle
Why this matters here: Bava Kamma 1:2 says the rule applies to נכסים. The Yerushalmi must define what counts as your property — and sancta sit on the boundary between mine and Hashem's.

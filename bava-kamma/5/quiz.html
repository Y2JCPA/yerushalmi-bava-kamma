QUESTION 1 OF 5
If A digs a pit 10 tefachim and B deepens it by one, who is liable for damage according to R' Yitzchak?
A. Only the last digger (B)
B. Only the first digger (A)
C. Both A and B — אַחַר שְׁנֵיהֶן לִנְזָקִין
D. Neither, since each one is partial
Correct: C — R' Yitzchak reads the beraita: latter for death, but אַחַר שְׁנֵיהֶן לִנְזָקִין — both for damage.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How can a plasterer become liable for someone else's pit?
A. By making it deeper
B. When the digger said סוֹד אֶת הַבּוֹר הַזֶּה וּקְנֵה אוֹתוֹ — plastering effects acquisition
C. Automatically, since he touched the pit
D. He never can — only digging counts
Correct: B — שְׁמִירַת נְזָקִין כִּשְׁמִירַת קִנְיָין: when plastering is the agreed קנין act, it makes him owner — and therefore liable.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the shiur of a pit לְהָמִית versus לְהַזִּיק?
A. Both 10 tefachim
B. Death: 10 tefachim; damage: כָּל שֶׁהוּא
C. Death: כָּל שֶׁהוּא; damage: 10 tefachim
D. Both כָּל שֶׁהוּא
Correct: B — The Yerushalmi states clearly: לְהָמִית עֲשָׂרָה טְפָחִים, וּלְהַזִּיק כָּל שֶׁהוּא.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is R' Elazar haKappar's measure for a pit?
A. Three deep, four wide
B. Ten tefachim minimum
C. כִּמְלוֹאוֹ שֶׁלַּנּוֹפֵל — fitted to the falling animal, chicken or camel
D. Any size
Correct: C — R' Elazar haKappar's relative shiur: the pit is sized to whatever animal falls in.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is R' Shimon's principle for which sancta count under 'against his neighbor and he lied'?
A. Only קדשי קדשים
B. Only valid sancta
C. Sancta for which the owner bears אחריות
D. All sancta equally
Correct: C — R' Shimon: when the owner is responsible for replacement (אחריות), the sancta are read as בָּעֲמִיתוֹ וְכִחֵשׁ; otherwise בַּיי וְכִחֵשׁ.

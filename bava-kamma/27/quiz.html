Yerushalmi Bava Kamma 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is R' Yehoshua's principle for the four cases of 'exempt but heaven holds you'?
A. All four involve indirect or moral wrongs that beit din can't compel
B. All four are forbidden mid'rabanan
C. Each requires a kohen's involvement
D. Each was a takanah of Yavneh
Correct: A — אֵין חַיָּיבִין לְשַׁלֵּם מִן הַדִּין וְאֵין הַשָּׁמַיִם מוֹחֲלִין — exempt in court because the harm is indirect, but heaven keeps the debt open.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When are robbers who let out a flock liable, according to Rav Hoshaya?
A. Always, regardless of intent
B. Only when they release it to steal it (לגוזלה)
C. Only when no witnesses are present
D. Only on weekdays
Correct: B — בְּשֶׁהוֹצִיאוּהָ לְגוֹזְלָהּ — robbery transfers responsibility; pure malicious release does not.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does an animal that fell into a garden pay?
A. Full damage
B. Half damage
C. מַה שֶׁנֶּהֱנֵית — only what it benefited
D. Triple damage
Correct: C — An accidental fall is reduced to a benefit-restitution standard, not full tort liability.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish, when is a grazing animal first liable?
A. From the first bite
B. Once it crosses into a different bet-se'ah
C. Only after it leaves the field, enters רשות הרבים, and then enters another field
D. Only if seen by witnesses
Correct: C — אֵינָהּ חַיֶיבֶת עַד שֶׁתֵּצֵא מִתּוֹכָהּ לִרְשׁוּת הָרַבִּים וְתִיכָּנֵס מֵרְשׁוּת הָרַבִּים לְשָׂדֶה אֲחֶרֶת.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Mishnah measure 'what it damaged' in a field?
A. By weighing what was eaten
B. שָׁמִין בֵּית סְאָה — value before vs. after
C. By comparing market prices
D. Always 25% of the field's value
Correct: B — שָׁמִין בֵּית סְאָה בְּאוֹתָהּ שָׂדֶה כַּמָּה הָיְתָה יָפָה וְכַמָּה הִיא יָפָה — proportional value loss.

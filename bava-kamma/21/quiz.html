Yerushalmi Bava Kamma 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, what does וּבַעַל הַשּׁוֹר נָקִי exempt the owner from?
A. From paying full damages
B. From half the kofer (weregilt)
C. From the death penalty itself
D. From watching his bull
Correct: B — R' Eliezer says: נָקִי מֵחֲצִי כוֹפֶר. He reads the verse as exempting the tam-owner from half-weregilt.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is R' Akiva's challenge to R' Eliezer?
A. The verse is about a fire, not a bull
B. וַהֲלֹא מוּקְדָּם הוּא לְבֵית הַסְקִילָה חֲמוּרָה — the owner is already going to the stoning court
C. Half-kofer doesn't exist as a category
D. The owner of a tam never pays anything
Correct: B — R' Akiva argues that in the standard case the owner faces capital prosecution, so the verse cannot be focused on his money payments.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does R' Eliezer narrow the case to make his reading work?
A. Only when the bull is hekdesh
B. Only in cases where the owner declared the bull ownerless
C. Only when the bull intended to kill an animal but ended up killing a person
D. Only on Shabbat
Correct: C — לֹא אָמַרְתִּי אֶלָּא בְּנִתְכַּוֵון לַהֲרוֹג אֶת הַבְּהֵמָה וְהָרַג אֶת הָאָדָם — only in non-capital scenarios. R' Akiva ends up using the same narrowing.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the meaning of the Yerushalmi's pushback יַקְשֶׁינָּהּ עַל דִּידֵיהּ?
A. He should ask his own teacher first
B. Before objecting to R' Eliezer, R' Akiva should ask the same question against his own position
C. He should learn it as a Mishnah rather than a beraita
D. He should let R' Miasha decide
Correct: B — The Yerushalmi notes that R' Akiva's challenge would equally undermine his own reading, so he must accept the same narrowing — either via R' Miasha's solution (בְּשֶׁעָבַר וּשְׁחָטוֹ) or by adopting R' Eliezer's qualification.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Yerushalmi prefer learning הַשּׁוֹר יִסָּקֵל from הַשּׁוֹר יִסָּקֵל rather than from סָקוֹל יִסָּקֵל הַשּׁוֹר?
A. Doubled verbs are always weaker
B. מוּטָּב יִלָּמֵד הַשּׁוֹר יִסָּקֵל מִשּׁוֹר יִסָּקֵל — exact phrasing match is preferred over a near-match
C. The doubled form is in a different sefer
D. R' Akiva rejects all gezerot shavot
Correct: B — The Yerushalmi establishes a meta-principle: when choosing between two possible source-phrases for a gezerah shavah, the one with identical phrasing wins over the doubled-language version.

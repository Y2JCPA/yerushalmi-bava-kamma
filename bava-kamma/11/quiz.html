5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does R' Yochanan explain the dog-with-pitta case?
A. Only if it lit every single ear of grain
B. It is like a thrown arrow (כְּזוֹרֵק אֶת הַחֵץ מִמָּקוֹם לְמָקוֹם)
C. Only if the dog is muad
D. The owner is exempt entirely
Correct: B — R' Yochanan treats the original ignition as a single act that propagates, like an arrow. Resh Lakish demands per-sheaf rekindling.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical halakha that Rav Yirmiyah reports in Rav's name about tam/mu'ad?
A. Always like R' Yehuda
B. Always like R' Meir
C. הֲלָכָה כְרִבִּי מֵאִיר בְּתַמָּה וּכְרִבִּי יוּדָה בְהַעֲדָאָה
D. הֲלָכָה כְרִבִּי יוּדָה בְּתַמָּה וּכְרִבִּי מֵאִיר בְהַעֲדָאָה
Correct: C — Practice follows R' Meir for the threshold of tam (three episodes) and R' Yehuda for testimony / he'adah (three days).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What halakhic dispute does the Yerushalmi use as a parallel to the gap-day goring case?
A. Tum'at met vs. tum'at sheretz
B. A niddah whose mid-day check was missed (Rav Ada bar Aḥawa vs. Rav Huna)
C. Mu'ad vs. tam in shor-gores-shor
D. Mishkav vs. moshav
Correct: B — Rav Ada bar Aḥawa says נִידָּה וַודַּאי; Rav Huna says נִדָּה סָפֵק — same gap-evidence question.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Yitzchak narrow the rule of אָדָם מוּעָד לְעוֹלָם?
A. Only when both people were already sleeping (שְׁנֵיהֶם יְשֵׁינִין)
B. Only when fully awake
C. Only on Shabbat
D. Only with vessels, not bodily injury
Correct: A — R' Yitzchak: if one came to sleep next to a sleeper, the joining party is the muad — he created the risk.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the new Mishnah (הַמַּנִּיחַ אֶת הַכַּד), who pays whom?
A. The walker pays for breaking the jar
B. The jar-owner pays for the walker's injury, and the walker is exempt for the jar
C. Both pay each other equally
D. Both are exempt

📖 Overview — Killer Oxen, Slaves, and Owner-less Bulls

וּבַעַל הַשּׁוֹר נָקִי. נָקִי מִדְּמֵי עֶבֶד. נָקִי מִדְּמֵי וְולָדוֹת.
The sugya: Daf 22 sweeps through three Mishnayot at the seam between Chapter 4 and Chapter 5. It works out what the owner is 'free' from when his bull kills, when intent matters, who counts as an 'owner' for liability, and what happens to a bull condemned to stoning whose verdict is reversed.

🧱 What 'ובעל השור נקי' Excludes

The Yerushalmi maps competing readings of the exemption clause.

👤 נקי מדמי עבד

  • Owner is exempt from paying for a slave
  • Even though a slave normally has 30 sela liability
  • Verse derives from 'כי ינצו אנשים' — people, not bulls

👶 נקי מדמי ולדות

  • Owner is exempt from paying for fetuses
  • Bulls that act unintentionally are exempt
  • Even if intentional — still no fetus payment

📜 Slave damages source

  • או בן יגח או בת יגח
  • ואם עבד — derives slave damages
  • Two opinions on extent of payment

💭 The dispute

  • R' Chiyya bar Va: addition can't exceed core
  • Other view: pays the entire damage
  • R' Yasa heard a teaching from R' Shmuel bar Yitzhak but forgot
Key move: The same three words 'ובעל השור נקי' generate two distinct exemptions depending on which baraita you follow.

💬 Bulls Without Owners — Who Pays?

The Mishnah lists six 'ownerless' bulls. R' Yehudah carves out three.
M
Mishnah: שור האשה, שור היתומים, שור אפיטרופוס, שור המדבר, שור ההקדש, שור הגר שמת — all subject to death penalty.
RY
R' Yehudah: the wild bull, the hekdesh bull, and the heir-less ger's bull are exempt — מפני שאין להם בעלים.
RH
R' Hoshaia: for damages R' Meir holds liable, R' Yehudah exempt; but everyone agrees on כופר — liable.
RY2
Some say (R' Yochanan in the name of R' Yannai): just as they argue about damages, they argue about kofer too.
Practical ruling: R' Zeira tells R' Yirmiyah: follow R' Hoshaia — disputed in damages, agreed in kofer.

🧠 Dedicating a Stoned Bull

When does hekdesh take effect on a bull condemned to die?
1️⃣ Bull rubs on a wall and kills — sentence not yet pronounced.
↓
2️⃣ Before sentencing: dedication is valid; if slaughtered, meat is permitted.
↓
3️⃣ After sentencing: dedication invalid; if slaughtered, meat is forbidden.
↓
4️⃣ Sentence creates a status of איסור הנאה that blocks normal acquisition.
↓
5️⃣ If verdict is reversed: R' Yochanan vs Reish Lakish on whether ownership reverts.
Bottom line: The legal effect of a death-sentence is so strong that even hekdesh cannot override it once the verdict is final.

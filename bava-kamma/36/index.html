📖 Overview — Honor, Insult, and Forgiveness

אַף עַל פִּי שֶׁהוּא נוֹתֵן לוֹ אֵינוֹ נִמְחָל לוֹ עַד שֶׁיְּבַקֵּשׁ מִמֶּנּוּ שֶׁנֶּאֱמַר וְעַתָּה הָשֵׁב אֵשֶׁת הָאִישׁ כִּי נָבִיא הוּא
The sugya: Daf 36 spans three Mishnayot. First: vulnerable parties (deaf-mute, minor, woman, slave) — פגיעתן רעה: dangerous to meet. Second: the famous fixed-price list for slaps and indignities, R' Akiva's ruling that all Jews are aristocrats, and the trickster who broke an oil flask to expose a woman's hair. Third: even after paying, the offender is not forgiven until he asks — and the wronged party may not be cruel.

💄 The Flask of Oil — A Famous Trick

How a defendant tried to prove a woman's 'honor' was already cheap.
1️⃣ A man uncovered a woman's hair; R' Akiva ruled אַרְבַּע מֵאוֹת זוּז.
↓
2️⃣ The defendant asks for time to pay — and gets it.
↓
3️⃣ He waits at her courtyard and breaks a flask of oil (worth an אִיסָּר) before her.
↓
4️⃣ She uncovers her own hair to gather the oil — he plants witnesses.
↓
5️⃣ He returns to R' Akiva: 'Should I really pay 400 זוז to this woman?'
↓
6️⃣ R' Akiva: הַחוֹבֵל בְּעַצְמוֹ אַף עַל פִּי שֶׁאֵינוֹ רַשַּׁאי פָּטוּר וַאֲחֵרִים שֶׁחָבְלוּ בוֹ חַיָיבִים — self-injury exempt, others liable.
Bottom line: Compare to הַקּוֹצֵץ אֶת נְטִיעוֹתָיו — cutting your own trees: not allowed, but no liability. Others who cut them are liable. Honor works the same way.

🤐 בושת and Verbal Insult

Three sub-rulings the Yerushalmi attaches to the price list.

💧 Spit That Misses

  • Mishnah: רָקַק וְהִגִּיעַ בּוֹ הָרוֹק
  • Question: how far must it reach? body? clothes?
  • הַמְבַיֵישׁ אֶת חֲבֵירוֹ בִּדְבָרִים פָּטוּר

👴 Insulting a זקן

  • Reish Lakish: הַמְבַיֵישׁ אֶת הַזָּקֵן נוֹתֵן לוֹ דְמֵי בוֹשְׁתּוֹ מֻשְׁלַם
  • Story: someone insulted R' Yehuda b. Chanina
  • Reish Lakish fined him a לִיטְרָא דִדְהַב — pound of gold

❓ 'I Don't Know' Defense

  • הֲרָגַתָּה שׁוֹרִי קָצָצְתָּה נְטִיעוֹתַיי
  • Defendant: אֵינִי יוֹדֵעַ
  • R' Yochanan: still חייב — when witnesses testify
Practical reading: The Yerushalmi inserts these halakhot inside the bigger Mishnah on indignities — verbal, gestural, and accusatorial all get separate treatment.

👑 R' Akiva on the Dignity of Every Jew

The Mishnah's most quoted line — and its theological core.
M
Anonymous Mishnah: הַכֹּל לְפִי כְבוֹדוֹ — payment scales with the victim's status.
RA
R' Akiva: אֲפִילוּ עָנִי שֶׁבְּיִשְׂרָאֵל רוֹאִין אוֹתָן כְּאִילּוּ הֵן בְּנֵי חוֹרִין שֶׁיָּרְדוּ מִנִּכְסֵיהֶן — even the poorest is treated as a fallen aristocrat.
RA
Reason: שֶׁהֵן בְּנֵי אַבְרָהָם יִצְחָק וְיַעֲקֹב.
Y
Application: in the woman's case, R' Akiva imposed the full 400 זוז regardless of her socioeconomic standing.
Why it matters: R' Akiva democratizes the price list. Honor is not aristocratic privilege but Israelite birthright.

🙏 The Mishnah on Forgiveness

Even after paying, the offender is not yet forgiven.
Mishnah: אַף עַל פִּי שֶׁהוּא נוֹתֵן לוֹ אֵינוֹ נִמְחָל לוֹ
↓
Source 1: וְעַתָּה הָשֵׁב אֵשֶׁת הָאִישׁ כִּי נָבִיא הוּא — Avimelech must ask Avraham
↓
Offender
Pay damages + ask forgiveness
If refused
Rav: יַעֲשֶׂה שׁוּרַת בְּנֵי אָדָם וִיפַיְיסֶנּוּ — assemble a row to ask again
Wronged party
שֶׁלֹּא יְהֵא הַמּוֹחֵל אַכְזָרִי — must not be cruel
↓
R' Yose: הוֹצִיא לוֹ שֵׁם רַע אֵין לוֹ מְחִילָה עוֹלָמִית — defamation has no eternal pardon
Bottom line: Job's friends model the cycle: prayer for the offender brings the prayer's own restoration — וַיי שָׁב אֶת שְׁבוּת אִיּוֹב בְּהִתְפַּלְּלוֹ בְּעַד רֵעֵהוּ.

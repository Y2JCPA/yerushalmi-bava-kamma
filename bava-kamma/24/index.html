📖 Overview — The Courtyard, the Pots, and the Pregnant Woman

הִכְנִיס פֵּירוֹתָיו לַחֲצַר בַּעַל הַבַּיִת שֶׁלֹּא בִּרְשׁוּת... וְאִם הוּזְּקָה בָהֶן בַּעַל הַפֵּירוֹת חַיָיב.
The sugya: Daf 24 walks through three Mishnayot of Chapter 5 that share a single structural test: was it brought in with permission? The answer flips liability between the courtyard-owner and the item-owner. The daf closes by pivoting to the unintentional miscarriage — and to the question of whether a fetus can be freed by 'tooth and eye'.

🤝 Rebbi vs the Sages — What 'With Permission' Means

R' Yirmiyah maps where everyone agrees and where they fight.

🏠 House

  • Everyone agrees
  • 'הכנס' implies 'and I will watch it'
  • Owner of bayit fully liable

🌾 Field

  • Everyone agrees
  • 'הכנס' implies 'and you watch it'
  • Owner of items watches himself

🏡 Courtyard

  • Where they fight!
  • Rebbi: courtyard is like a field
  • Sages: courtyard is like a house

🔒 Special case

  • R' Yose b' R' Bun: courtyard with a lock
  • Treat as field for sheaves
  • Lock implies guarding is on the owner
Halakhah: Rav Yehuda in Shmuel's name and Rav Ze'ira: הלכה כרבי. But Rebbi himself adds: 'in all of these — only if he explicitly accepted to watch.'

💬 The Pregnant Woman Caught in the Crossfire

A bull or a man intends to hit someone — and a pregnant woman miscarries.
M
Mishnah: a bull aiming at another bull, hit a woman, fetus lost — פטור מדמי וולדות.
M2
But: a man aiming at a friend, hit a pregnant woman — משלם דמי וולדות to the husband.
AB
Abba bar Rav Hana: the bull case is taught only as setup for the human case — bulls never pay fetus damages either way.
ABY
Abba Yose ben Hanan: 'הרה' teaches he must hit the place of pregnancy. If he hit hand or foot — פטור.
RU
R' Uqba: what if it's incest — is the perpetrator a 'בעל'? The verse says 'בעל' — only one who could be called husband.
Drasha: From כי ינצו אנשים — humans not bulls. The Yerushalmi reads each word of Exodus 21:22 to filter who pays whom.

👁️ Fetuses and 'Tooth and Eye'

R' Yasa's rule about freeing a Canaanite slave's fetus.
1️⃣ Canaanite slave goes free if owner knocks out his tooth or eye.
↓
2️⃣ R' Yasa in R' Yochanan's name: עוברין אין יוצאין בשן ועין.
↓
3️⃣ Question: ניחא בשן — but how does an unborn fetus have an 'eye' to lose?
↓
4️⃣ R' Yose b' R' Bun: the baraita means — fetuses don't go free by their mother's tooth and eye.
↓
5️⃣ Therefore: damage to the mother does not propagate freedom to the fetus.
Bottom line: Daf 24 closes one principle and opens another: ownership and intention determine liability — but separate persons (mother / fetus) follow separate emancipation rules.

📖 Overview — Why Each Av Gets Its Own Pasuk

אִילּוּ לֹא נֶאֱמַר שׁוֹר הָיִיתִי לָמֵד שׁוֹר מִן הַבּוֹר... וְלָמָּה תְנִינָתָהּ הָכָא. דְּאִית לֵיהּ מִילִּין סַגִּין
The sugya: R' Bun bar Chiyya, in the name of R' Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak, runs a kal va-chomer between שור and בור and shows that either could have been derived from the other. The Yerushalmi answers: דְּאִית לֵיהּ מִילִּין סַגִּין — the Torah states each one explicitly because each category carries its own unique halakhot that a derivation could not generate. The sugya then brings two parallel cases — משכב/מושב and the parshiyot of נירות / שילוח טמאין — to show that this hermeneutic move is a general principle.

🔁 R' Bun bar Chiyya's Kal Va-chomer

Try to derive the bull from the pit (and the pit from the bull).
If 'שור' had not been written
I would learn it from בור
↓
מָה אִם הַבּוֹר שֶׁאֵין דַּרְכּוֹ לֵילֵךְ וּלְהַזִּיק חַיָּיב לְשַׁלֵּם
שׁוֹר שֶׁדַּרְכּוֹ לֵילֵךְ וּלְהַזִּיק לֹא כָּל שֶׁכֵּן
↓
Direction A
Bor pays full damage
→ so should shor
Direction B (פרכא)
Shor (tam) pays half damage
→ maybe bor should also pay only half?
↓
Same kal va-chomer runs in both directions — neither category is fully provable from the other
Why this matters: The Yerushalmi is not really trying to eliminate one of the avot. It uses the kal va-chomer to ask the deeper question: why does the Torah waste words by writing both?

💬 So Why Does the Torah Write Both?

The Yerushalmi's quick, sharp answer.
Q
If shor and bor can each be derived from the other, וְלָמָּה תְנִינָתָהּ הָכָא? — why does the Torah write both?
A
דְּאִית לֵיהּ מִילִּין סַגִּין — because each av carries many unique halakhot a derivation could never generate.
Y
This is a hermeneutical principle: a kal va-chomer is overruled when each side has independent details the other side lacks.
Yerushalmi style: Not every kal va-chomer needs to win. Sometimes the Torah writes both terms precisely because each one needs its own halakhic life.

🪞 Parallel Cases of the Same Move

The Yerushalmi shows this is a general principle, not just nezikin.

🐂 שור / 🕳️ בור

  • Each could be derived from the other
  • Bor pays full damage, shor (tam) pays half
  • Each has unique laws → both must be written

🛏️ משכב / 🪑 מושב

  • Mishkav of a zav: 4 tefachim makes it tamei
  • Moshav of a zav: 1 tefach makes it tamei
  • Each could be learned from the other, yet both written

🕯️ נירות / שילוח טמאין

  • Two distinct parshiyot in Bamidbar
  • Both share: מִיַּד וּלְדוֹרוֹת
  • Each section adds halakhot the other lacks
The shared pattern: When two parallel pesukim could each be derived from the other, the Torah keeps both because each carries מילין סגין — its own unique laws. This is exactly the third middah of R' Yishmael: a כלל that is taught from one parsha.

📐 Mishkav vs. Moshav — A Closer Look

The Yerushalmi's tightest illustration of the rule.
SurfaceMinimum Size for Tum'ahDistinct Halakhah
מוֹשָׁב (seat)טֶפַח אֶחָדSmaller surface area still becomes tamei
מִשְׁכָּב (bed)אַרְבָּעָה טְפָחִיםBigger surface needed because of intended use
Common featureBoth are bnei meshamesh adamCarry tum'at zav — but each via its own pasuk
Why both pesukim: Knowing only mishkav, you might think nothing smaller than 4 tefachim becomes tamei. Knowing only moshav, you might think any tefach-sized item suffices. The Torah needs both to fix the shiur exactly.

🤕 R' Yose's Ruling on Sequential Damage

A halakhic landing point at the end of the daf.
1️⃣ A person injures his fellow first.
↓
2️⃣ Later, that same person becomes injured himself.
↓
3️⃣ Question: does the later injury reduce or cancel his liability?
↓
4️⃣ Source: וּמַכֶּה בְהֵמָה יְשַׁלְּמֶנָּה — read as יְשַׁלֵּם פְּחָתָהּ.
↓
5️⃣ Ruling: he still pays for the original injury, even after he himself was hurt.
Bottom line: Liability is fixed at the moment of damage. A later change to the מזיק's own status does not unwind what he already owes.

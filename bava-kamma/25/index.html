📖 Overview — Defining the Pit

מַה הַבּוֹר שֶׁהוּא כְדֵי לְהָמִית עַד עֲשָׂרָה טְפָחִים. אַף כָּל דָּבָר שֶׁיֵּשׁ בּוֹ כְדֵי לְהָמִית עַד עֲשָׂרָה טְפָחִים
The sugya: Mishnah 5:6 and 5:7 unfold the rules of the bor — first defining when a hole counts as a death-hazard (10 tefachim), then asking who pays in a partnership pit. The Yerushalmi anchors both pesukim of Shemot 21 (יפתח and יכרה) into a system, then asks the natural follow-up: if you didn't dig but acquired the pit, are you still on the hook? Plus the practical question — how thick a cover counts as 'proper'?

🪜 The 10-Tefach Death Threshold

How the Mishnah extends 'pit' to all hazardous excavations.

🕳️ בור / שיח / מערה

  • Pit, ditch, cave
  • All count as bor
  • 10 tefachim → death-level

📐 חריצין / נעיצין

  • Trenches and pole-bases
  • Same liability framework
  • Mishnah generalizes to any excavation

📏 Below 10 Tefachim

  • Animal falls in and dies → פָּטוּר
  • Animal merely injured → חַיָיב
  • Two regimes: death vs. damage
Why 10: The Mishnah uses bor as the paradigm because it's the case the Torah names. The number 10 is then projected outward: anything that יֵשׁ בּוֹ כְדֵי לְהָמִית at that depth carries the same liability.

💬 Two Pesukim — Two Kinds of Pit

How the Yerushalmi splits death-liability and damage-liability.
Y
כְּתִיב כִּי יִפְתַּח אִישׁ בּוֹר וְכִי יִכְרֶה אִישׁ בּוֹר — two pesukim, one for the pit-of-damages and one for the pit-of-death.
R' Yitz
Both are derived from a single source. But they diverge in halakhah: פָּטוּר עַל הַכֵּלִים in the death-pit and חַיָיב עַל הַכֵּלִים in the damage-pit.
Y
And by the second pasuk's word אוֹ כִי יִכְרֶה — we add anyone who acquired the pit (bought, inherited, given as a gift), not only the one who dug it.
Key move: Liability for a bor doesn't depend on the act of digging. The Torah's two verbs (פתיחה and כריה) anchor two regimes; the word אוֹ reaches even the passive owner of a pit.

🤝 The Partnership Pit

When two people share a pit, who pays?
How can a pit have two partners?
↓
R' Yannai: שֶׁנָּתְנוּ שְׁנֵיהֶן אֶבֶן הָאַחֲרוֹנָה — they together placed the final stone that opened it to 10 tefachim
↓
Neither covered
First passes, doesn't cover. Second passes, doesn't cover. → Second liable
First covered, second uncovered
→ Second liable
First covered, second found uncovered, didn't recover
→ Second liable
↓
Liability transfers when the pit is left open by the most recent person who could have acted
Cedar logic: R' Yochanan: we give the second partner enough time to לִכְרוֹת אֲרָזִים מִן הַלְּבָנוֹן — to cut cedars from Lebanon. The window is reasonable, but proximity to Lebanon makes it stricter or more lenient.

📊 Liability Matrix for the Pit

The Mishnah's edge cases.
CaseAnimalVessels
Animal falls in, properly covered✗ פטור✗ פטור
Animal falls in, improperly covered✓ חייב✗ פטור
Animal falls forward from sound of digging✓ חייב—
Animal falls backward from sound of digging✗ פטור—
Cheresh / shoteh / katan ox falls in✓ חייב—
Son, daughter, slave, slave-girl falls in✗ פטור—
Asymmetry: The pit is liable for animals but never for vessels — and never for capable humans. The Yerushalmi reads this from the verse's words: שׁוֹר אוֹ חֲמוֹר, not 'a person.'

🪨 What Counts as 'Properly Covered'?

The Yerushalmi gives a real-world test.
1️⃣ Mishnah: כִּיסָּהוּ כָּרָאוּי — covered properly. But what does that mean?
↓
2️⃣ Yerushalmi: יָיבֹא כַּיי דְּתַנִּינָן תַּמָּן — same as we said elsewhere.
↓
3️⃣ Standard: כְּדֵי שֶׁתְּהֵא עֲגָלָה מֲהַלֶּכֶת טְעוּנָה אֲבָנִים
↓
4️⃣ Meaning: a wagon loaded with stones can drive across without breaking through.
Practical takeaway: The cover isn't just visual — it has to bear weight equivalent to the heaviest realistic load on a public road. That's the bar between liability and exemption when an animal still falls in.

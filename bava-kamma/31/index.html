📖 Overview — Composite Acts and Composite Witnesses

גָּנַב עַל פִּי שְׁנַיִם וְטָבַח וּמָכַר עַל פִּיהֶם אוֹ עַל פִּי שְׁנַיִם אֲחֵרִים מְשַׁלֵּם תַּשְׁלוּמֵי אַרְבָּעָה וַחֲמִשָּׁה
The sugya: The daf has two Mishnayot. The first lists acts that still incur ד׳ וה׳ even when other complicating factors are present — Shabbat, idolatry, Yom Kippur, the father's death, even slaughter for dogs. R' Shimon dissents on two cases. The Halakhah introduces R' Bun bar Chiyya's principle of כְּדֵי רִשְׁעָתוֹ: when two penalties are due, the court selects one. The second Mishnah turns to perjured witnesses — מה משלמין when one or both pairs are zomemim.

The pattern: Even when the slaughter or sale is itself a sin (Shabbat, idolatry, Yom Kippur), the Mishnah still imposes ד׳ וה׳. The act is criminally complete for theft purposes.

💬 Why Shouldn't These Cases Be Exempt?

The Yerushalmi raises the principle of כְּדֵי רִשְׁעָתוֹ.
Q
Did not R' Abbahu say in the name of R' Yochanan: הֵזִיד בְּחֵלֶב וְשָׁגַג בְּקָרְבָּן — he is warned, lashed, and brings a sacrifice? So why not double penalty here too?
RB
R' Bun bar Chiyya in the name of R' Shmuel bar R' Yitzchak: כְּדֵי רִשְׁעָתוֹ (Deut. 25:2). Two penalties before the court — pick one.
Y
But heavenly penalties (like a קרבן) are not 'before the court' — so the principle of one-penalty-only does not exclude them.
The exegetical move: שְׁנֵי דְבָרִים מְסוּרִין לְבֵית דִּין הַתּוֹפֵס אֶחָד מֵהֶן. When two court-penalties collide, you take one; but a court-penalty + a heavenly-penalty can both stand.

🔪 R' Shimon's Two Exemptions

Two slaughters that, for him, do not count as 'slaughter' for ד׳ וה׳.

🐕 לרפואה / לכלבים

  • Slaughter for medicine or dog-food
  • R' Shimon: not the normal purpose
  • Doesn't trigger ד׳ וה׳

🩸 שחט ונמצא טריפה

  • Animal turns out non-kosher post-slaughter
  • R' Shimon: a treif slaughter doesn't count
  • Doesn't trigger ד׳ וה׳

✅ Tanna kamma

  • The act of שחיטה is the trigger, regardless of purpose
  • Even שוחט חולין בעזרה pays
  • R' Shimon פוטר בשני אילו
What divides them: The Tanna kamma views שחיטה as a discrete act of disposing of the stolen animal. R' Shimon requires that the slaughter actually achieve its halakhic point (kosher meat, normal use).

👥 Mishnah 7:3 — Zomemim Witnesses

Who pays whom, and how much, when witnesses are exposed as perjurers.
Two pairs of witnesses:
גניבה + טביחה/מכירה
↓
Both pairs zomemim
First pair pays כפל
Second pair pays תשלומי שלושה
Only second pair zomemim
He still pays כפל;
they pay תשלומי שלושה
One of second pair zomem
בָּטְלָה עֵדוּת שְׁנִיָיה
second testimony nullified
One of first pair zomem
בָּטְלָה כָל הָעֵדוּת
everything falls — אם אין גניבה אין טביחה
↓
The structure mirrors the act: theft is the foundation; without it, slaughter and sale collapse.
Logical anchor: שֶׁאִם אֵין גְּנֵיבָה אֵין טְבִיחָה וְאֵין מְכִירָה — slaughter/sale presuppose theft. Knock out theft, knock out everything.

🧮 The Math of Triple Restitution

Why do the second-pair zomemim pay 'תשלומי שלושה'?
1️⃣ Total liability for stealing + slaughtering an ox = 5×.
↓
2️⃣ Of that, 2× is for the theft alone.
↓
3️⃣ The slaughter/sale witnesses tried to impose the additional 3×.
↓
4️⃣ Therefore when only the second pair is perjured, they pay the 3× they tried to inflict.
↓
5️⃣ תשלומי שלושה = the increment from 2× to 5×.
Bottom line: כאשר עשו — what they tried to do to him is what they pay. Each pair is liable only for the slice of damage their false testimony was attempting to add.

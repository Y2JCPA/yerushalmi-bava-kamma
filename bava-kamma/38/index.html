📖 Overview — Repentance, Transformation, and the Craftsman

גָּזַל מָרִישׁ וּבְנָאוֹ בַבִּירָה. בֵּית שַׁמַּי אוֹמְרִים. יְקַעְקֵעַ כָּל הַבִּירָה כוּלָּהּ וְיִתְּנֶינּוּ לוֹ. וּבֵית הִלֵּל אוֹמְרִים. יִתֵּן לוֹ דָמָיו מִפְּנֵי תַקָּנַת שָׁבִים
The sugya: Daf 38 surveys a series of cases where the gezeilah has changed since the theft. Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel famously dispute the stolen beam built into a building — Hillel rules in favor of תקנת שבים. R' Yaakov bar Acha grounds Hillel's position in יישוב ארץ ישראל. The amoraim then carve up the rules for aged animals and slaves, spoiled fruit, invalidated coins, leavened matter through Pesach, and artisans who break what they were hired to repair.

🏛️ The Stolen Beam Built into a Building

Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel split on what the penitent must restore.
BS
Beit Shammai: יְקַעְקֵעַ כָּל הַבִּירָה כוּלָּהּ וְיִתְּנֶינּוּ לוֹ — he must tear down the whole building and return the actual beam.
BH
Beit Hillel: יִתֵּן לוֹ דָמָיו מִפְּנֵי תַקָּנַת שָׁבִים — he just pays its value, to support the penitents.
RYbA
R' Yaakov bar Acha in the name of R' Yehoshua ben Levi: the reason is also יִישׁוּב אֶרֶץ יִשְׂרָאֵל — settlement of the Land.
RNbY
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak: would we really refuse a man who put his bundle in another's ruin temporarily? Once he heard the ארץ ישראל reason — that's the answer.
Why this matters: The halakhah is not pure restitution. It is calibrated by social policy: encourage teshuva, encourage building the land.

🐂 When Does 'הרי שלך לפניך' Apply?

R' Meir vs. the Tanna Kamma — and the amoraic refinements.

📜 Mishnah

  • Animal aged → pays כשעת הגזילה
  • Slaves aged → pays כשעת הגזילה
  • R' Meir: for slaves only — 'הרי שלך לפניך'

🐄 Rav Huna

  • Stole calf → became bull = pays
  • Stole bull → it aged = 'הרי שלך לפניך'
  • Drawing the line at species-level change

🐂 Shmuel

  • Even calf → bull, says 'הרי שלך לפניך'
  • More lenient than Rav Huna
  • Halakhah: like R' Meir (Rav Yehudah)

✂️ Rav Karna

  • Stole calf → became bull → slaughtered/sold
  • Pays ד' or ה' of the calf's value
  • Penalty tracks the original gezeilah
R' Zeira & Rav Chisda: Stole fat → became thin: must replace the fat. Stole thin → fattened: returns it as is. The gazlan never benefits, never overpays.

🔨 Artisans, Builders, and the Last Nail

Mishnah 9:4 — when does the craftsman become the responsible party?
1️⃣ Owner gives item to artisans to repair and they ruined it → liable to pay.
↓
2️⃣ Carpenter receives chest/box/cupboard and breaks it → liable.
↓
3️⃣ Builder hired to take down a wall and damages stones → liable.
↓
4️⃣ Wall collapsed on the other side → exempt. But if from the pounding (מַחֲמַת הַמַּכָּה) → liable.
↓
5️⃣ Rav Huna: only if he drove in the last nail — קָבַע בּוֹ מַסְמֵר הָאַחֲרוֹן כְּדֵי לְזַכּוֹת לְכֵלָיו.
The principle: Liability transfers when the artisan acquires craftsman's rights in the object — symbolized by the final nail. Until then, his actions are framed as the owner's loss.

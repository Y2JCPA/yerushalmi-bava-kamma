📖 Overview — Konam Vows and the Stolen Ger

הָאוֹמֵר לִבְנוֹ קוֹנָם שֶׁאַתָּה נֶהֱנֶה לִי אִם מֵת יוֹרִישֶׁנּוּ. בְּחַיָּי וּבְמוֹתִי אִם מֵת לֹא יוֹרִישֶׁנּוּ
The sugya: The daf opens with a father's konam against his son's enjoyment, and asks how 'בחיי ובמותי' is different from saying each phrase alone. R' Yose answers with a parallel from Nedarim: a demonstrative word like 'בית זה' or 'נכסים אילו' binds the property both during life and after death. The sugya then turns to one who robbed a ger and swore falsely: when the ger dies with no relatives, the keren and chomesh go to the kohanim and the asham to the altar. The Amoraim debate whose Mishnah this follows — R' Yose HaGalili's reading or R' Akiva's.

🚫 Three Konam Phrasings — Three Outcomes

R' Jeremiah's puzzle: why does 'בחיי ובמותי' alone block inheritance?

🟢 בחיי alone

  • Konam binds only during the father's life
  • When father dies → son inherits
  • Death dissolves the prohibition

🟡 במותי alone

  • Phrasing bound to time of death
  • Still: אִם מֵת יִירָשֶׁנּוּ
  • Inheritance is not 'הנאה' from the father

🔴 בחיי ובמותי

  • Combined phrase binds both periods
  • Son cannot inherit
  • Property goes to brothers/sons or to creditors
R' Jeremiah's question: מַה בֵין אָהֵן דָּמַר חָדָא חָדָא לָהֵן דָּמַר תַּרְתֵּי תַּרְתֵּי — what is the difference between saying it one-by-one and saying it as a pair?

💬 R' Yose: Nedarim vs. Nezikin

The demonstrative word 'this' / 'these' is what binds beyond life.
RJ
Rav Jeremiah and R' Yose ben Ḥanina: קוֹנָם לְבַיִת זֶה שֶׁאֵינִי נִכְנַס בְחַיַּי וּבְמוֹתִי — once he said 'this house', he forbade it both in life and after death.
RY
R' Yose: תַּנִּינָן בִּנְזָקִין מַה דְלֹא תַנִּינָן בִּנְדָרִין — we taught in Neziqin what we didn't teach in Nedarim.
RY
קוֹנָם הֲנָיַית נְכָסַיי אֵילּוּ עָלַי בְּחַיַּי וּבְמוֹתִי — once he says 'these' my properties, he forbids them on himself in life and after death.
Y
The trigger word is the demonstrative — זה / אילו — which fixes the prohibition onto the object itself rather than onto the time period.
Yerushalmi insight: Nezikin makes explicit a halakhah that Nedarim left implicit: words of demonstration bind property permanently.

⛪ Mishnah 9:10 — Mishmar Division of the Asham

Who gets the money, who gets the offering?
1️⃣ Money to Yehoiarib + asham to Yedaiah → he has fulfilled his obligation.
↓
2️⃣ Asham to Yehoiarib + money to Yedaiah — if the offering is still alive, Yedaiah's people offer it.
↓
3️⃣ Otherwise: he must bring another asham.
↓
4️⃣ Rule: הַמֵּבִיא גְזֵילוֹ עַד שֶׁלֹּא הֵבִיא אֲשָׁמוֹ יָצָא — bringing the stolen item before the asham works; the reverse does not.
↓
5️⃣ נָתַן אֶת הַקֶּרֶן וְלֹא נָתַן אֶת הַחוֹמֶשׁ אֵין חוֹמֶשׁ מְעַכֵּב — the chomesh is not me'akev.
Bottom line: The robber must restore the principal before atonement; the chomesh is a side-payment that doesn't block kapparah.

📖 Overview — Stretching the Neck and the Burning Sheaves

רַב אָמַר. עָקְמָה צַוָּארָהּ וְאָכְלָה מְשַׁלֵּם מַה שֶּׁהִזִּיקָה
The sugya: Daf 10 has only three segments but they are dense. Rav rules that an animal that bent its neck to eat — even from the doorway of a store — pays for full damage, not just for benefit. The Yerushalmi resolves the apparent contradiction with our Mishnah by re-reading the Mishnah's case as a donkey carrying lambs that stretched their necks while passing. Then Rav teaches the principle of אָכְלָה שְׂעוֹרִים מְשַׁלֵּם תֶּבֶן — payment is calibrated to benefit, not to what the animal ate. The daf closes with the new Mishnah of jumping dogs/goats and the dog-with-hot-pitta case, which the next daf will unpack.

🦒 Rav: The Bent Neck Pays Full

Rav reads the Mishnah aggressively — and creates a problem.
Rav: עָקְמָה צַוָּארָהּ וְאָכְלָה — מְשַׁלֵּם מַה שֶּׁהִזִּיקָה
If the animal bent its neck and ate, the owner pays for damage (not just benefit)
↓
Objection: but our Mishnah says מִפֶּתַח הֶחָנוּת מְשַׁלֵּם מַה שֶּׁנֶּהֱנֵית
From the doorway it pays only what it benefited!
↓
R' Yose b. R' Bun: תִּיפְתָּר בְּשֶׁהָיָה חֲמוֹר טָעוּן גְּדָיִים
Re-read the Mishnah: a donkey carrying lambs, and as it walks past, the lambs stretch their necks
↓
Doorway
מַה שֶּׁנֶּהֱנֵית
only benefit
Inside store
מַה שֶּׁהִזִּיקָה
full damage
The reconciliation: When it is the animal itself that bends its neck deliberately, Rav treats this as an unusual move and assigns full damage. The Mishnah's softer rule applies only to a passive scenario — a donkey carrying its load, lambs grazing as they pass.
4 / 9

🌾 Rav's Principle: Pay by the Benefit, Not the Item

אָכְלָה שְׂעוֹרִים מְשַׁלֵּם תֶּבֶן
1️⃣ The animal eats expensive barley (שְׂעוֹרִים).
↓
2️⃣ Rav: the owner pays for cheap straw (תֶּבֶן) — the benefit it would have gotten anyway.
↓
3️⃣ R' Chiyya disagrees: if it ate wheat (חִיטִּין), which is bad for it, the owner is exempt entirely.
↓
4️⃣ But if the animal changed from its normal feed (שִׁינָּת וְאָכְלָה), liability returns to full damage.
↓
5️⃣ Final rule: payment tracks real benefit. When the animal eats abnormally, it crosses into full liability.
Bottom line: The Yerushalmi separates 'normal eating in a public space' (pay benefit only) from 'unusual feeding' (pay full damage). Rav and R' Chiyya disagree on edge cases like wheat, but the structural distinction holds.

📦 Three Container Cases at the End of the Halakhah

When the food is exposed to the public, what counts as 'inside'?

🎒 Basket on the back

  • קוּפָּתוֹ מוּפְשֶׁלֶת לַאֲחוֹרָיו
  • Cow stretches and eats from it
  • נֶזֶק שָׁלֵם

🚪 Container half in / half out

  • סוֹג שֶׁנָּתוּן בְּפֶתַח הֶחָנוּת
  • Cow stretches and eats from it
  • נֶזֶק שָׁלֵם

🛣️ Open public square

  • אָכְלָה מִתּוֹךְ הָרְחָבָה
  • Animal grazes in the open
  • מַה שֶּׁנֶּהֱנֵית — only benefit
The pattern: A container — even a half-out one — counts as a private domain to its contents. The animal that reaches into it pays full damage, not benefit. Only fully exposed food in the public square triggers the lenient benefit-rule.

💬 Did the Mishnah Say 'Jumped' or 'Fell'?

The next Mishnah (2:5) opens with a textual variant.
M
הַכֶּלֶב וְהַגְּדִי שֶׁקָּפְצוּ מֵרֹאשׁ הַגַּג וְשִׁיבְּרוּ אֶת הַכֵּלִים מְשַׁלְּמִין נֶזֶק שָׁלֵם מִפְּנֵי שֶׁהֵן מוּעָדִין
T1
שֶׁקָּפְצוּ — they jumped.
T2
שֶׁנָּפְלוּ — they fell.
R' Yo
כֵּינִי מַתְנִיתָא. שֶׁקָּפְצוּ. נָפְלוּ לֹא בְדָא — the correct text is 'jumped'. 'Fell' does not apply (i.e. there is no full liability for an accidental fall).
Why this matters: Jumping is a deliberate act — the animal acted out its 'mu'ad' nature. Falling is accidental — closer to a passive bor case. R' Yochanan locks the Mishnah to the deliberate version, so full liability rests on intent, not just outcome.

🔥 Setup for Daf 11 — The Dog and the Hot Pitta

The Mishnah ends with a famous dual-liability case.
כֶּלֶב שֶׁנָּטַל אֶת הַחֲרָרָה וְהָלַךְ לוֹ לְגָדִישׁ... עַל הַחֲרָרָה מְשַׁלֵּם נֶזֶק שָׁלֵם וְעַל הַגָּדִישׁ מְשַׁלֵּם חֲצִי נֶזֶק
The case: A dog grabs a hot pitta and runs to a stack of sheaves. While eating the pitta, it ignites the stack. For the pitta, נֶזֶק שָׁלֵם (the dog ate it directly). For the stack, חֲצִי נֶזֶק (the fire spread indirectly). Daf 11 will pick this up: Resh Lakish demands fire-ignition contact at every single sheaf; R' Yochanan rules נַעֲשֶׂה כְּזוֹרֵק אֶת הַחֵץ — the dog is like one who shoots an arrow.

QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does Rav rule about an animal that bent its neck and ate (עָקְמָה צַוָּארָהּ וְאָכְלָה)?
A. It pays only what it benefited
B. It is exempt entirely
C. It pays מַה שֶּׁהִזִּיקָה — full damage
D. It depends on the time of day
Correct: C — Rav: bending the neck is a deliberate, non-passive act, so the lenient benefit-rule does not apply.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does R' Yose b. R' Bun reconcile Rav with the Mishnah's 'doorway = benefit'?
A. He says Rav is wrong
B. He re-reads the Mishnah as a donkey carrying lambs that stretched their necks (חֲמוֹר טָעוּן גְּדָיִים)
C. He distinguishes between Shabbat and weekday
D. He limits Rav to wheat only
Correct: B — R' Yose: תִּיפְתָּר בְּשֶׁהָיָה חֲמוֹר טָעוּן גְּדָיִים וּבִשְׁעַת עֲבָרָתָן פָּשְׁטוּ צַוָּארֵיהֶן.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If an animal ate barley (שעורים), how does Rav say the owner pays?
A. Pay for barley
B. Pay for straw (תֶּבֶן)
C. Pay double
D. Exempt
Correct: B — אָכְלָה שְׂעוֹרִים מְשַׁלֵּם תֶּבֶן — payment is keyed to the benefit, not the item eaten.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is R' Yochanan's textual ruling on the new Mishnah (2:5)?
A. Both 'jumped' and 'fell' apply
B. כֵּינִי מַתְנִיתָא. שֶׁקָּפְצוּ. נָפְלוּ לֹא בְדָא
C. Only 'fell' is correct
D. He emends the Mishnah to 'walked'
Correct: B — R' Yochanan: the correct text is 'jumped' — full liability requires deliberate movement. 'Fell' would not apply.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dog-and-hot-pitta case, how does the Mishnah split the payment?
A. Full for both
B. Half for both
C. Full for the pitta, half for the stack
D. Half for the pitta, full for the stack
Correct: C — עַל הַחֲרָרָה מְשַׁלֵּם נֶזֶק שָׁלֵם וְעַל הַגָּדִישׁ מְשַׁלֵּם חֲצִי נֶזֶק. Direct eating = full; spreading fire = half.

Yerushalmi Bava Kamma 39 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, what is the Mishnah's case of נוֹתֵן לוֹ דְּמֵי צַמְרוֹ?
A. Specifically when chemicals (סממנין) burned the wool
B. When the vat itself burned it
C. Only when the dyer was negligent
D. When the customer changed his mind
Correct: A — Rav Huna says: שֶׁהִקְדִּיחוּ סַמְמָנִין, but if the vat burned it, the dyer can say הרי שלך לפניך. Shmuel disagrees and says even with chemicals he says הרי שלך לפניך.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the worked example, the wool would have been worth 25 minas if dyed red, but only 20 minas dyed black. The dyer was paid 10 minas in wages. What does the dyer get?
A. 10 minas (full wages)
B. 5 minas (the שבח, since it is less than הוצאה)
C. Nothing
D. 25 minas
Correct: B — The Mishnah says: when הוצאה (10) exceeds שבח (5), the dyer receives only the שבח. The customer says אַתְּ אוֹבַדְתְּ דִּידָךְ.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is R' Yonah's case extending the dyer Mishnah?
A. A shepherd who lost a sheep
B. An agent given 8 dinars to buy wheat in Tiberias who buys in Sepphoris instead
C. A worker who quit mid-job
D. A borrower who damaged a tool
Correct: B — R' Yonah says וְתִשְׁמַע מִינֵּהּ — derive from the dyer that the deviating agent absorbs the gap (25 modii expected, 20 actual).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On the wheat-for-barley case where the agent bought the wrong grain and the price rose, what does R' Yehudah hold?
A. Agent keeps all the profit
B. Principal keeps all the profit
C. Profit is split between agent and principal
D. The sale is void
Correct: C — אִם הוֹתִירוּ הוֹתִירוּ לִשְׁנֵיהֶן — R' Yehudah says they share. R' Nasa explains: הוֹאִיל וּבָאָת לוֹ הֲנָיָיה מִתַּחַת יָדוֹ.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does R' Meir say is the seller's intent when the agent deviates and buys barley instead of wheat?
A. לֹא נִתְכַּוֵון הַמּוֹכֵר לְזַכּוֹת אֶלָּא לַלּוֹקֵחַ
B. לֹא נִתְכַּוֵון הַמּוֹכֵר לְזַכּוֹת אֶלָּא לְבַעַל הַמָּעוֹת
C. The sale is invalid
D. It depends on the price
Correct: A — R' Meir: the seller transferred ownership only to the agent (the actual buyer in front of him), so any profit belongs to the agent. R' Yehudah inverts this — to בעל המעות.

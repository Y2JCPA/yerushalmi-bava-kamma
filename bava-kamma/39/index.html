📖 Overview — The Dyer, the Agent, and the Deviation

נָתַן צֶמֶר לַצַּבָּע וְהִקְדִּיחֳהּ הַיּוֹרָה נוֹתֵן לוֹ דְּמֵי צַמְרוֹ
The sugya: The Mishnah in Bava Kamma 9:5 handles three cases of a craftsman or agent who botches the job: the vat burns the wool, the wool comes out poorly dyed, or the wool is dyed the wrong color. The Yerushalmi sharpens the dispute between Rav Huna and Shmuel about whether 'הרי שלך לפניך' applies, calculates שבח-vs-הוצאה with concrete numbers, and ports the rule to a parallel agency case (Tiberias vs. Sepphoris wheat). It concludes by exploring R' Meir vs. R' Yehudah on profit-sharing when an agent buys the wrong grain.

🎨 Three Mishnaic Cases of a Dyer Gone Wrong

The Mishnah of 9:5 sets up a tight ladder of fault.

🔥 הקדיחה היורה

  • The vat burned the wool entirely
  • Dyer pays דְּמֵי צַמְרוֹ
  • Total loss — pure value of the wool

🟫 צבעו כאור (poorly dyed)

  • Wool exists but quality is reduced
  • If שבח > הוצאה → pay the expenses
  • If הוצאה > שבח → pay the smaller value-added

🎨 wrong color

  • Asked for אדום, dyed שחור (or vice versa)
  • R' Meir: נוֹתֵן לוֹ דְּמֵי צַמְרוֹ
  • R' Yehudah: same שבח/הוצאה calculation as case 2
Key move: The Mishnah treats the three cases on a sliding scale: total destruction → defect → deviation. Only on case 3 do R' Meir and R' Yehudah split.

💬 Rav Huna vs. Shmuel — When Can the Dyer Say 'הרי שלך לפניך'?

The Halakhah opens with a sharp two-way split.
RH
Rav Huna: the Mishnah's case is שֶׁהִקְדִּיחוּ סַמְמָנִין — the chemicals burned the wool. But if הִקְדִּיחָה יוֹרָה, the vat itself burned it, the dyer can simply say הֲרֵי שֶׁלָּךְ לְפָנֶיךָ.
Sh
Shmuel: אֲפִילוּ הִקְדִּיחוּ סַמְמָנִין — even if the chemicals burned it, he says 'here is yours before you.' Either way, no cash payment for the wool itself.
Y
The dispute is about how to read the Mishnah's נוֹתֵן לוֹ דְּמֵי צַמְרוֹ: does it mean cash, or does it mean 'returns the burnt wool as-is'?
Conceptual stakes: Rav Huna distinguishes between a defect in the dyer's tool (vat) and a defect in the materials (chemicals). Shmuel collapses both — the wool is what the wool is.

🧮 Working the Numbers — שבח vs. הוצאה

The Yerushalmi gives a concrete worked example.
1️⃣ Customer supplies 5 lots of wool + 5 portions of dye and pays the dyer 10 minas in wages.
↓
2️⃣ Customer says: dye it red. Dyer dyes it black.
↓
3️⃣ Hypothetical red value: הֲוָה טָב עֶשְׂרִין וַחֲמִשָּׁה מָנוֹי — would have been worth 25 minas.
↓
4️⃣ Actual black value: לֵית הוּא טָב אֶלָּא עֶשְׂרִין מָנוֹי — worth only 20 minas.
↓
5️⃣ Customer says: אַתְּ אוֹבַדְתְּ דִּידָךְ. אֲנָא לָא אוֹבֵד דִּידִי — 'You lose yours, I do not lose mine.' Dyer eats the loss.
The principle: The customer's wool was worth 15 (value of materials before work). It is now worth 20. The שבח is 5 minas, less than the 10 minas in wages. The dyer recovers only 5 — the smaller of שבח/הוצאה.

📊 Wheat-for-Barley — R' Meir vs. R' Yehudah on Profit-Sharing

When the agent buys the wrong commodity, who pockets a price gain?
ScenarioR' MeirR' Yehudah
Price dropsLoss falls on agentLoss falls on agent
Price risesProfit goes to agent aloneProfit is split between agent and principal
Reasoningלֹא נִתְכַּוֵון הַמּוֹכֵר לְזַכּוֹת אֶלָּא לַלּוֹקֵחַלֹא נִתְכַּוֵון הַמּוֹכֵר לְזַכּוֹת אֶלָּא לְבַעַל הַמָּעוֹת
Why split (R' Yehudah)—הוֹאִיל וּבָאָת לוֹ הֲנָיָיה מִתַּחַת יָדוֹ — the agent benefited via the principal's money, so he shares
R' Nasa's resolution: If the agent fulfilled his agency, the seller transferred ownership to the principal. If he deviated, ownership goes to the agent — but profit-sharing is required because אָסוּר לִיהֲנוֹת מֵחֲבֵירוֹ.

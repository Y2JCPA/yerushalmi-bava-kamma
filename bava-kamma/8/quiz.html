Yerushalmi Bava Kamma 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What case did R' Abba bar Mamal bring before R' Hoshaia and R' Yudan Nesia?
A. An ox that gored another ox
B. A mule that wagged its tail and broke something (כִּישְׁכְּשָׁה בִּזְנָבָהּ)
C. A chicken that pecked dough
D. A dog that jumped from a roof
Correct: B — The Yerushalmi opens: כִּישְׁכְּשָׁה בִּזְנָבָהּ. כְּגוֹן אִילֵּין פֵּירְדָתָא. מָהוּ?
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Hoshaia, when must the owner literally grab the animal's tail?
A. Always
B. Never — only watch from a distance
C. Only when the tail-wagging is the animal's normal behavior (דַּרְכָּהּ לָכֵן)
D. Only on Shabbat
Correct: C — R' Hoshaia: if it is unusual behavior, no need to grab; but בְּשֶׁדַּרְכָּהּ לָכֵן צָרִיךְ שֶׁיְּהֵא תוֹפֵשׂ בִּזְנָבָהּ.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If an animal stepped on a skin (נוד) full of oil, what does R' Yirmiyah rule?
A. Full damages for both
B. Half for both
C. Full for the skin, half for the oil
D. Half for the skin, full for the oil
Correct: C — עַל הַנּוֹד מְשַׁלֵּם נֶזֶק שָׁלֵם וְעַל הַשֶּׁמֶן מְשַׁלֵּם חֲצִי נֶזֶק — direct contact pays full, indirect breakage pays half.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the ricochet case (table in רה"ר → glass shatters in רה"י), what is the Yerushalmi's question?
A. Whether to follow the stepping (דְּרִיסָתָהּ) or the breaking (שְׁבִירָתָהּ)
B. Whether tooth or foot is more lenient
C. Whether the owner was negligent
D. Whether the animal was tame or notorious
Correct: A — אַחַר מִי אַתְּ מְהַלֵּךְ אַחַר דְּרִיסָתָהּ אוֹ אַחַר שְׁבִירָתָהּ? If we follow stepping → exempt; if breaking → liable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the new Mishnah at the end of daf 8 (2:2)?
A. Four avot of damages
B. הַתַּרְנוֹגַלִּין מוּעָדִין לְהַלֵּךְ כְּדַרְכָּן וּלְשַׁבֵּר
C. אָדָם מוּעָד לְעוֹלָם
D. Tame vs. notorious bull
Correct: B — The daf ends by introducing the chicken Mishnah, which sets up the next sugya about tied threads (דְלִיל קָשׁוּר) and scraping motion.

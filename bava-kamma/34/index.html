📖 Overview — Five Categories of Bodily Damage

הַחוֹבֵל בַּחֲבֵירוֹ חַיָיב עָלָיו מִשּׁוּם חֲמִשָּׁה דְבָרִים בְּנֶזֶק בְּצַעַר בְּרִיפּוּי בְּשֶׁבֶת וּבְבוֹשֶׁת
The sugya: The daf finishes the perek-7 cleanup (forbidden trade, the dangerous dog, pigeon-trap distances) and then opens perek 8 — the framework of bodily damage. The famous Mishnah lists five categories: נזק, צער, ריפוי, שבת, בושת. The Yerushalmi shows how they stack (5/4/3/2/1 depending on the injury), and confronts the eye-for-an-eye verses head-on, proving that the Torah requires monetary payment, not literal mutilation.

🚫 Trade in Forbidden Items

Two pesukim, two prohibitions — and a key distinction.

📜 The doubling of pesukim

  • טְמֵאִים הֵם לָכֶם
  • וּטְמֵאֵים יִהְיוּ לָכֶם
  • Yerushalmi: one for אכילה, one for הנאה / סחורה

✅ Rabbinically forbidden

  • מוּתָּר לַעֲשׂוֹת בּוֹ סְחוֹרָה
  • Trade is permitted
  • Used for non-eating purposes

❌ Torah-forbidden

  • אָסוּר לַעֲשׂוֹת בּוֹ סְחוֹרָה
  • But: the donkey and the camel?
  • Answer: לִמְלַאכְתּוֹ הוּא גָדֵל — they are raised for work, not as merchandise
How the rule is salvaged: The donkey and camel seem to disprove the rule, but the Yerushalmi answers that they are raised for labor, not as goods to be traded — so the principle still stands.

🕊️🐕 Settlement Animals — Dogs and Pigeons

Two final perek-7 cases sharpen what 'protecting the public' means.
M
לֹא יְגַדֵּל אָדָם אֶת הַכֶּלֶב אֶלָּא אִם כֵּן קָשׁוּר בְּשַׁלְשֶׁלֶת — no dog without a chain.
RY
R' Yose b. R' Chanina: about anyone who raises a vicious dog the verse says לַמָּס מֵרֵעֵהוּ חָסֶד — he withholds kindness from his neighbor.
Q
Pigeons: here we say 30 ריס from a settlement, but elsewhere only 50 אמה. Which is it?
RY
R' Yose b. R' Bun: לִרְעוֹת מְרֵיעָה נֹ אַמָּה. לִפְרוֹחַ פּוֹרַחַת אֲפִילוּ אַרְבָּעַת מִילִין — they feed within 50 amah but fly up to four mil. The two distances measure two different ranges.
Reading principle: Apparent contradictions in shiurim usually point to two different physical realities. Feeding range and flying range need different protection radii.

📊 The Five Categories of Damage — and How They Stack

From the beraita: 5 / 4 / 3 / 2 / 1 — based on the type of blow.
Type of BlowCategories OwedSpecific Heads
Hit on the hand and severed it5נֶזֶק, צַעַר, רִיפּוּי, שֶׁבֶת, בּוֹשֶׁת
Hit on the hand and it swelled4צער, ריפוי, שבת, בושת (no permanent נזק)
Hit on the head, swelled (visible)3צער, ריפוי, שבת
Place not visible2צער, ריפוי
With a tome (folder/papers) in his hand1בּוֹשֶׁת only
What the gradation shows: Each category has its own trigger. נזק requires a permanent loss of capacity (assessed as if he were sold as a slave). בושת can be triggered even without any physical contact at all — a humiliating gesture is enough.

👁️ Why עין תחת עין Must Mean Money

The Yerushalmi's tight derivation from the doubling of שוגג and מזיד.
Two verses: עַיִן תַּחַת עַיִן + לֹא תָחוֹס עֵינֶךָ
↓
One refers to שוגג, one refers to מזיד — both must be stated
↓
Only שוגג stated?
Then I'd say מזיד actually has his hand cut off
Only מזיד stated?
Then I'd say שוגג pays nothing
Both stated
Both pay מָמוֹן
↓
Limit case: a blind man who blinded another, an amputee who severed an arm — הֵיאַךְ זֶה מִתְקַיֵים וַעֲשִׂיתֶם לוֹ כַּאֲשֶׁר זָמַם?
↓
מַגִּיד שֶׁאֵינוֹ מְשַׁלֵּם אֶלָּא מָמוֹן — therefore the Torah's measure must be money.
The clincher: If the Torah's penalty were literal mutilation, it would fail in cases where the perpetrator has nothing to lose (a blind man, an amputee). The Torah's principle of measure-for-measure only works if the payment is monetary.

💸 How to Assess צער and ריפוי

Three voices on appraising pain — and an aside on a Roman.
1️⃣ R' Ze'ira: ask the victim — how much would you pay to avoid this pain? That's the figure.
↓
2️⃣ Mar Ukva: the Mishnah's wording is כַּמָּה אָדָם רוֹצֶה לִיטּוֹל — how much would he take to accept this pain? The roles are flipped, but the number is similar.
↓
3️⃣ Ben Azai: include נִכְאֵי מָזוֹן — convalescent food. Before, lentils sufficed; now only eggs and chicken. The mazik pays the upgrade.
↓
4️⃣ R' Yose b. Yaakov's vignette: a Roman soldier wouldn't go to battle until he had eaten back the days he'd missed. Pain restitution mirrors the body's own recovery economy.
↓
5️⃣ ריפוי from the next Mishnah: includes complications (צמחים) and re-injuries (חָיְיתָה וְנִסְתְּרָה) caused by the original blow.
Bottom line: The Yerushalmi treats damages as a recovery package, not a flat fee. Pain assessment, food upgrades, and ongoing medical care all flow from the original act of injury.

QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Yerushalmi say the Torah doubled טְמֵאִים הֵם לָכֶם with וּטְמֵאֵים יִהְיוּ לָכֶם?
A. To repeat for emphasis
B. One forbids eating, one forbids הנאה (and so trade)
C. One refers to live animals, one to carcasses
D. To exclude rabbinically-forbidden items
Correct: B — אֶחָד אִיסּוּר אֲכִילָה וְאֶחָד אִיסּוּר הֲנָיָיה. Anything Torah-forbidden is also forbidden for trade; rabbinic prohibitions allow trade. The donkey/camel objection is answered by 'they are raised for work, not as merchandise.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yose b. R' Bun, why does one Mishnah say 30 ריס for pigeon traps and another 50 אמה?
A. The numbers are alternative texts
B. 50 אמה is the feeding range, 30 ריס (or 4 mil) is the flight range
C. 30 ריס applies to Eretz Yisrael only
D. The 50 אמה refers to the dovecote itself
Correct: B — לִרְעוֹת מְרֵיעָה נֹ אַמָּה. לִפְרוֹחַ פּוֹרַחַת אֲפִילוּ אַרְבָּעַת מִילִין — feeding range vs. flight range explain the two figures.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If someone hits another on the head and it swells visibly, how many categories of payment apply?
A. Five
B. Four
C. Three: צער, ריפוי, שבת
D. One: בושת
Correct: C — Per the beraita: head + swelling visible = 3 (no נזק because there's no permanent loss of capacity assessed כעבד נמכר; no בושת unless there's an embarrassment context). Five only when a limb is severed.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Yerushalmi's strongest proof that עַיִן תַּחַת עַיִן means money?
A. A gezerah shavah from שבת
B. The case of a blind man who blinds another, or an amputee who severs an arm — literal mutilation can't be applied uniformly
C. Because the Torah always means money
D. Because the Sanhedrin abolished bodily punishments
Correct: B — אִם הָיָה סוּמֶא וְסִימָּא אֵת עֵינוֹ... הֵיאַךְ זֶה מִתְקַיֵים וַעֲשִׂיתֶם לוֹ כַּאֲשֶׁר זָמַם. The principle of measure-for-measure breaks down unless the payment is monetary — מַגִּיד שֶׁאֵינוֹ מְשַׁלֵּם אֶלָּא מָמוֹן.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Ben Azai's נִכְאֵי מָזוֹן refers to what?
A. A fine for delayed payment
B. Convalescent food upgrades — e.g., previously lentils sufficed, now only eggs and chicken
C. A discount for partial healing
D. Compensation for missed work only
Correct: B — Ben Azai adds that the mazik pays for the dietary upgrades the victim now needs — בְּקַדְמִיתָא הֲוִינָא אֲכִיל טְלוֹפְחִין וְיָרָק. וּכְדוֹן לֵית בִּי מֵיכַל אֶלָּא בֵּיעִין וְתַרְנוֹגַלִּין.

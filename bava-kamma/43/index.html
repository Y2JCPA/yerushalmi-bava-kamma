📖 Overview — Yi'ush, Rescue, and Switched Property

נָטְלוּ מוֹכְסִין אֶת חֲמוֹרוֹ וְנָתְנוּ לוֹ חֲמוֹר אַחֵר... הֲרֵי אֵילּוּ שֶׁלּוֹ מִפְּנֵי שֶׁהַבְּעָלִין מִתְיָיאֲשִׁין מֵהֶן
The sugya: Daf 43 covers the second half of Chapter 10's mishnayot on rescue and recovery. The sugya develops yi'ush (the owner's despair) as the legal mechanism that transfers ownership when publicans seize a donkey, robbers take a garment, or a swarm of bees flies away. It then turns to recognizing stolen vessels in another's hand, and the moral economy of pouring out one's own wine to save another's honey — and water vs. honey in the desert.

🔄 Where Were the Vessels Switched?

The Mishnah distinguishes between an artisan's shop and a public gathering.

🛠️ At the Artisan

  • יִשְׁתַּמֵּשׁ בָּהֶן — may use them
  • Then go out and search for his own
  • Artisan implicitly authorizes use

🕯️ Mourner's House / Wedding

  • אַל יִשְׁתַּמֵּשׁ בָּהֶן — may NOT use
  • Just go out and search for his own
  • No authorization implied in such gatherings

🏪 Bar Qora's Shop

  • R' Hiyya's example via R' Abba bar Hanan
  • כָּל הַנּוֹטֵל מֵאֶצְלוֹ נוֹטֵל בִּרְשׁוּת
  • Every taking and depositing is by permission
Logic: Use is permitted only where the holder of the items has implicit authority to grant it.

🐝 The Swarm of Bees and Joshua's Stipulation

May the beekeeper enter another's field to retrieve his swarm?
M
Mishnah: he may enter the field and take his swarm. If he caused damage, he pays — אֲבָל לֹא יָקוֹץ אֶת הַסּוֹכָה, but he may not cut a branch on condition to pay.
RY
R' Yishmael b. R' Yochanan b. Beroka: he may even cut and pay.
Y
It is a stipulation of the court — שֶׁעַל מְנָת כֵּן הִנְחִיל יְהוֹשֻׁעַ לְיִשְׂרָאֵל אֶת הָאָרֶץ — Joshua distributed the Land on this very condition.
RH
R' Hinena b. Pappos in R' Yochanan's name: only when taken in flight, and only immediately. If it left and returned, it was said only out of fear or seduction.
Big idea: Some property rules are not biblical — they are a stipulation built into the original distribution of the Land.

💧 Saving the Other's Cargo — Wages or Sale?

Two travelers on the road. The honey leaks. The water leaks in the desert.
1️⃣ Default: if he silently pours out his wine to save the honey, אֵין לוֹ אֶלָּא שְׂכָרוֹ — he gets only wages.
↓
2️⃣ But if he says אַצִּיל אֶת שֶׁלָּךְ וְאַתָּה נוֹתֵן לִי דְמֵי שֶׁלִּי — he gets the full value.
↓
3️⃣ In the desert, water beats honey: תְּנַיי בֵּית דִּין הוּא שֶׁיִּשְׁפּוֹךְ זֶה דִבְשׁוֹ — court stipulation forces the honey-holder to pour out for the water.
↓
4️⃣ A river sweeps two donkeys; if one abandons his to save the more valuable other's, only wages — unless he stipulates first.
↓
5️⃣ Yi'ush dispute: If he was crying 'I did not despair' — Reish Lakish says no yi'ush; R' Yochanan says חֲזָקָה מְיָיאֵשׁ הוּא — presumption of despair stands.
Principle: Words matter. Saving without a stipulation is volunteer work; saving with a stipulation is a contract.

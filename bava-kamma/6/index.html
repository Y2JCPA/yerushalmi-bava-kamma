📖 Overview — Whose Domain, Whose Damage

אַרְבָּעָה כְּלָלוֹת הָיָה רִבִּי שִׁמְעוֹן בֶּן אֶלְעָזָר אוֹמֵר מִשּׁוּם רִבִּי מֵאִיר בִּנְזָקִין
The sugya: Daf 6 walks the Mishnah's domain clauses with surgical care. R' Yirmiyah and R' Yose each show why נוֹהֲגִין הֵן בִּרְשׁוּת הַנִּיזָּק וְהַמַּזִּיק is not redundant — for one it includes the שומרים; for the other it excludes a courtyard belonging to neither. R' Yose b. Elazar's four-rule beraita then becomes the master grid for analyzing damages by domain access. The daf moves into Mishnah 1:3 (court procedure, women, joint liability) and opens Mishnah 1:4–1:5 — the count of five tam and five mu'ad, R' Tarfon's view of full damage in רשות הניזק, and which wild animals are notorious by default.

💬 Why נוֹהֲגִין בִּרְשׁוּת הַנִּיזָּק וְהַמַּזִּיק?

R' Yirmiyah and R' Yose split the redundancy.
RY
R' Yirmiyah: if the Mishnah only excluded the מזיק's private domain, I'd say liability applies even to a courtyard belonging to neither. So the extra clause comes to include the four שומרים.
RYo
R' Yose: that exclusion was already obvious. The extra clause comes to exclude a courtyard belonging to neither.
T1
Some Tannaim: shared courtyard is liable; courtyard of neither is exempt — מֵיטַב שָׂדֵהוּ.
T2
Some Tannaim: even a courtyard of neither is liable — וּבִעֵר בִּשְׂדֵה אַחֵר. מִכָּל מָקוֹם.
Yerushalmi style: Two amoraim disagree about what the same Mishnah clause is doing. Each reading then aligns with a different tannaitic camp.

💰 Mishnah 1:3 — Court Procedure

Each phrase narrows the appraisal procedure.
1️⃣ שָׁוֶה כֶסֶף — only mortgageable property (קרקע) is appraised. (Movables only if נִזָּק seized them.)
↓
2️⃣ בִּפְנֵי בֵית דִּין — only in court.
↓
3️⃣ עַל פִּי עֵדִים — only with witnesses.
↓
4️⃣ בְּנֵי חוֹרִין וּבְנֵי בְרִית — excludes Gentiles, slaves, and those disqualified for testimony.
↓
5️⃣ וְהַנָּשִׁים בִּכְלָל הַנֶּזֶק — women are in the system; R' Yishmael: וְאֵלֶּה הַמִּשְׁפָּטִים אֲשֶׁר תָּשִׂים לִפְנֵיהֶם (the verse must explicitly include them).
Bottom line: וְהַנִּיזָּק וְהַמַּזִּיק בְּתַשְׁלוּמִין — when both pay, each pays half. זֶה מַפְסִיד חֲצִי נֶזֶק וְזֶה מַפְסִיד חֲצִי נֶזֶק.

🐂 Mishnah 1:4 — Five Tam, Five Mu'ad

The Mishnah charts the default temperaments.

🟢 Five Tam Acts

  • נְגִיחָה — goring
  • נְגִיפָה — hitting
  • נְשִׁיכָה — biting
  • רְבִיצָה — lying down
  • בְּעִיטָה — kicking

🔴 Five Mu'ad

  • שֵׁן — eating what's appropriate
  • Mu'ad bull (after warnings)
  • Bull damaging in רשות הניזק
  • אדם — the human
  • (per R' Tarfon — see below)

⚖️ R' Tarfon

  • Bull goring in רשות הניזק
  • Pays full damage
  • Sages: half damage
  • R' Yochanan: our Mishnah is R' Tarfon's

🦁 Mishnah 1:5

  • Wolf, lion, bear, tiger, panther, snake — mu'ad
  • R' Eliezer: trained ones aren't mu'ad — except snake
  • R' Meir adds צבוע (hyena)
  • R' Yose b'R' Avin: only male hyena, dangerous as a lion
The thread: Tam vs. mu'ad isn't a static label — it tracks behavior. The Mishnah's five-and-five frame is then immediately complicated by R' Tarfon, R' Eliezer, and R' Meir.

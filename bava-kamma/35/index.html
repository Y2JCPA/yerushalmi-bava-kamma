📖 Overview — Working Through the Five Payments

רִבִּי יִרְמְיָה בָעֵי. עָלוּ בוֹ צְמָחִין סְבִיבוֹת הַמַּכָּה וְנִסְתְּרָה מַחֲמַת הַמַּכָּה מָהוּ. מִן מַה דִכְתִיב וְרַפֹּא יְרַפֵּא
The sugya: The daf continues the chapter on bodily damage, walking through ריפוי (medical costs), שבת (loss of earnings), and בושת (embarrassment). R' Yirmiyah asks about complications from a wound, R' Yitzchak distinguishes two types of שבת, and the sugya explores intent in בושת via the pasuk וְשָׁלְחָה יָדָהּ. The chapter then turns to slaves, contrasting the strict halakhic rule with R' Yochanan's personal practice of feeding his slave from his own meat and wine.

🥒 Two Kinds of שבת

R' Yitzchak resolves a tension in our Mishnah.

🥒 Permanent loss

  • Hand cut off — already paid נזק for the limb
  • רוֹאִין אוֹתוֹ כִּילּוּ חִיגֵּר יוֹשֵׁב וְשׁוֹמֵר קִישּׁוּאִין
  • Calculated only at the watchman wage

💼 Temporary disability

  • Burn, swelling, exposure to snow / cold water
  • Was earning a מנה? pays a מנה. סלע? pays a סלע
  • Real-time loss of full earnings

🩹 ריפוי still applies

  • בְּמָקוֹם שֶׁאֵינוֹ נִרְאֶה — even hidden injury
  • Snow / cold water count as injury
  • Independent of the שבת calculation
R' Yitzchak's resolution: תְּרֵין שִׁבְתִּין אִינּוּן — there are two distinct שבת frameworks: one for permanent loss already compensated, one for temporary disability still earning a wage.

💬 בושת and Intent

וְשָׁלְחָה יָדָהּ — does the wife of the בית דין marshal count?
M
Mishnah: נָפַל מִן הַגַּג וְהִזִּיק וּבִיֵישׁ — חַיָיב עַל הַנֶּזֶק וּפָטוּר עַל הַבּוֹשֶׁת — בושת requires intent.
T1
וְשָׁלְחָה יָדָהּ — but not the wife of a בית דין marshal (excluded).
T2
Another tanna: לְרַבּוֹת אֵשֶׁת שְׁלִיחַ בֵּית דִּין — including her!
Y
Resolution: excluded when he whipped with permission; included when he whipped without permission.
Practical reading: Authorized court-ordered injury creates no claim for the marshal's wife; rogue violence does. The same pasuk is read both ways depending on context.

🤲 R' Yochanan: Law vs. Mercy

A halakhic ruling and a personal practice in tension.
1️⃣ Halakhah: הַקּוֹטֵעַ יְדֵי עֶבֶד חֲבֵירוֹ — master collects all five payments.
↓
2️⃣ The amputated slave is supported מִן הַצְּדָקָה — by communal welfare.
↓
3️⃣ R' Acha: Israel is more obligated to support free people than slaves.
↓
4️⃣ But: רִבִּי יוֹחָנָן אֲכִיל קוֹפַּד וִיהִיב לְעַבְדֵּיהּ — R' Yochanan ate meat and gave to his slave.
↓
5️⃣ Resolution: תַּמָּן בְּמִידַּת הַדִּין. בְּרַם הָכָא בְּמִידַּת רַחֲמִים — there a rule of law, here a rule of mercy.
Bottom line: R' Yochanan recited הֲלֹא בְּבֶטֶן עוֹשֵׂינִי עֹשֵׂהוּ — 'did not the same Maker form us in the womb?' Halakhah sets a floor; piety builds higher.

QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does R' Yirmiyah ask regarding complications from a wound?
A. Whether the מזיק must pay if the patient dies
B. Whether growths around the wound (צְמָחִין סְבִיבוֹת הַמַּכָּה) keep the מזיק liable
C. Whether bandages count as ריפוי
D. Whether the doctor must be paid in advance
Correct: B — R' Yirmiyah asks about עָלוּ בוֹ צְמָחִין סְבִיבוֹת הַמַּכָּה וְנִסְתְּרָה מַחֲמַת הַמַּכָּה מָהוּ — the source וְרַפֹּא יְרַפֵּא is invoked.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does R' Yitzchak mean by תְּרֵין שִׁבְתִּין אִינּוּן?
A. There are two days of work each week
B. There are two distinct frameworks of שבת — permanent (שומר קישואין) and temporary (full wage)
C. There are two witnesses required for שבת
D. Two coins must be paid for each day of שבת
Correct: B — He distinguishes the case of an amputated limb (already paid) from temporary disability where one pays the actual lost wage.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Mishnah exempt one who fell from the roof from בושת?
A. Because falling is unavoidable
B. Because אֵינוֹ חַיָיב עַל הַבּוֹשֶׁת עַד שֶׁיְּהֵא מִתְכַּוֵין — בושת requires intent
C. Because there were no witnesses
D. Because the falling person is exempt from all damages
Correct: B — The Mishnah derives intent for בושת from וְשָׁלְחָה יָדָהּ וְהֶחֱזִיקָה בִּמְבוּשָׁיו.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If witnesses testify that someone blinded both of a slave's eyes simultaneously, what is the law?
A. The slave goes free and is paid for one eye
B. The slave goes free and is paid for both eyes
C. The slave goes free, but the מזיק pays nothing
D. The slave does not go free
Correct: C — סִימָּא שְׁתֵּי עֵינָיו כְּאַחַת... אֵינוֹ נוֹתֵן לוֹ כְּלוּם. Sequential blinding is different — first eye frees him, second eye must be paid in full.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Yerushalmi reconcile R' Yochanan's halakhic ruling with his personal generosity to his slave?
A. R' Yochanan changed his mind
B. תַּמָּן בְּמִידַּת הַדִּין. בְּרַם הָכָא בְּמִידַּת רַחֲמִים — strict law there, mercy here
C. His slave was actually a free man
D. He was wealthy and could afford it
Correct: B — R' Yochanan recited הֲלֹא בְּבֶטֶן עוֹשֵׂינִי עֹשֵׂהוּ. The halakhah sets a floor; the rule of mercy goes beyond it.

📖 Overview — Assessing Payment and the Duty to Guard

כָּל שֶׁחַבְתִּי בִשְׁמִירָתוֹ הִכְשַׁרְתִּי אֶת נִזְקוֹ
The sugya: The daf opens with two complementary derashot — R' Chanina anchors damage-assessment in מַכֶּה בְהֵמָה יְשַׁלְּמֶנָּה, and Bar Pedayah anchors limits in אִם טָרֹף יִטָּרֵף. R' Nasa shows each is needed: neither alone would generate the right rule. The sugya then tackles אֵין שָׁמִין — that ganav, gazlan, and (for Shmuel's expanded view) sho'el do not get the appraisal-discount that nezakin do. The daf closes by opening Mishnah 1:2: liability flows from שמירה, and R' Chiyya's beraita maps that onto שור and בור — but pointedly omits אש.

💬 R' Chanina vs. Bar Pedayah

Two derashot that together fix how damage is paid.
RḤ
מַכֶּה בְהֵמָה יְשַׁלְּמֶנָּה — read as יְשַׁלֵּם פְּחָתָהּ. The mazik pays the diminution in value.
BP
אִם טָרֹף יִטָּרֵף יְבִיאֵהוּ עֵד — bring the carcass and you don't pay for that case. There are situations of full exemption.
RN
Both quotes are needed: each one alone would generate the wrong rule for the other case.
Yerushalmi style: Two seemingly independent statements turn out to be one halakhic system. The Yerushalmi loves showing that each pasuk does work the others can't.

🔎 R' Nasa's Tzricha

Why each statement carries its own load.
Two derashot on damage payment
↓
Only R' Chanina?
I'd say: for הֶכְשֵׁר נְזָקִין pay nothing; for נִזְקֵי גוּפָה pay only פְּחָתָהּ
Only Bar Pedayah?
I'd say: for הֶכְשֵׁר נְזָקִין pay פְּחָתָהּ; for נִזְקֵי גוּפָה pay אֶת הַכֹּל
↓
Both together: צוֹרֶךְ שֶׁיֵּאָמֵר דְּרִבִּי חֲנִינָה וְצוֹרֶךְ שֶׁיֵּאָמֵר דְּבַר פְּדָיָיה
Bottom line: R' Nasa shows the two derashot interlock — neither generates the full rule alone. Classic Yerushalmi tzricha logic.

🏷️ אֵין שָׁמִין — Who Gets the Appraisal Discount?

Rav Yehudah b'shem Shmuel cuts the list down sharply.

🐂 Nezakin

  • Yes — שמין
  • Pay the diminution, not the original value
  • The carcass goes to the נִזָּק

🥷 Ganav / Gazlan

  • No appraisal
  • Source: חַיִים שְׁנַיִם יְשַׁלֵּם — alive, not dead
  • Robber: וְהֵשִׁיב אֶת הַגְּזֵילָה כַּאֲשֶׁר גָּזַל

🤝 Sho'el

  • Shmuel's expanded view: also no appraisal
  • וַאֲנִי אוֹמֵר. אַף לַשּׁוֹאֵל אֵין שָׁמִין
  • וְאַבָּא מוֹדֶה לִי

🛡️ Shomrin

  • וְהַשּׁוֹמְרִין כִּנְזָקִין הֵן
  • Watchmen are appraised like damage cases
  • Rav Chisda: נַעֲשֶׂה עִיקָּר טְפֵילָה
Key move: The Yerushalmi sorts liability profiles by their Torah source — and discovers that the appraisal discount is calibrated to the verb the Torah uses for that liability.

🛡️ Mishnah 1:2 — Liability via שמירה

The new Mishnah reframes the avot as guard-duties.
1️⃣ Mishnah: כָּל שֶׁחַבְתִּי בִשְׁמִירָתוֹ הִכְשַׁרְתִּי אֶת נִזְקוֹ.
↓
2️⃣ Even partial enabling — הִכְשַׁרְתִּי בְּמִקְצָת נִזְקוֹ — locks in full liability.
↓
3️⃣ R' Chiyya's beraita: this rule covers שור ובור.
↓
4️⃣ וְהָאֵשׁ לֹא תָנָה — fire is conspicuously absent.
↓
5️⃣ R' Yose explains: fire is לְהֶכְשֵׁר נְזָקָיו — it triggers the five בעל חובל categories (נזק, צער, ריפוי, שבת, בושת).
Why it matters: The Mishnah shifts the framework from object-categories to guarding-duty. Whatever you must guard — when it damages — you pay. Fire is excluded from the שמירה list because its liability flows through a different door.
